## First-class mode names

Use these routing labels in outputs and dashboards: `management_ir`, `expert_call`, `pm_internal_review`, `investment_committee`, `client_update`, `sell_side_call`, `earnings_call`, `investor_day`, `portfolio_watchlist_review`, `model_review`, `research_kickoff`, and `post_meeting_follow_up`.

All modes should rank questions by decision impact and include what not to ask/say, evidence requests, likely pushbacks, follow-up actions, and the next owning workflow.

## 1. Management or issuer meeting
Objective: test the company story, management credibility, KPIs, quality of growth, risks, and what evidence is needed next.

Must know:
- business model, product, customer base, market, unit economics, revenue quality, margin profile, cash flow, capex, working capital, and management incentives;
- what the model or memo assumes;
- issuer or management claims that need verification.

Question focus:
- growth drivers and sustainability;
- customer concentration, churn, pipeline, pricing, retention, cohort behavior;
- gross margin and operating leverage drivers;
- non-recurring revenue, add-backs, revenue recognition, backlog, bookings, ARR/MRR, NRR/GRR as relevant;
- cash conversion, working capital, capex, debt, covenants, liquidity;
- what management would cut first in a downside case.

Live-use output for a public-company management or IR meeting:
- lead with the investor stance, purpose of the conversation, and the evidence or disclosure that would change the view;
- place a compact conversation-flow sequence before the detailed must-ask question cards so the brief can be used during the meeting;
- make three or four questions the must-ask sequence and move additional diligence topics to `If Time Permits`;
- for each must-ask, concisely specify what answer to listen for, the clean follow-up if the answer stays qualitative or evasive, and the model or thesis implication;
- keep the public-disclosure boundary explicit: do not seek intra-quarter performance, customer-specific contract terms, undisclosed pricing, or other material nonpublic information;
- keep background to the handful of disclosed facts necessary to make the call plan useful, and route broader post-print or pre-print analysis to the relevant earnings workflow.

Useful companion skills: `company-tearsheet`, `financial-source-of-truth`, `financials-normalizer`, `earnings-preview`, `earnings-deep-dive`, `equity-model-update`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `thesis-tracker`, `sector-context-overlay`.

## 2. Research, PM, or client update
Objective: prepare the user to explain the current view, defend the evidence, update a recommendation, or clarify next analytical steps with a PM, client, or research team.

Must know:
- current thesis, recommendation or stance, source posture, model status, catalysts, risks, and decision-maker concerns;
- relevant comps, valuation range, estimates, key KPI changes, market setup, and peer read-throughs;
- what the user should ask for to sharpen the research, model, or client discussion.

Question focus:
- what changed since the prior view;
- key estimate, valuation, and catalyst debates;
- model and source gaps;
- risk controls, hedges, or sizing constraints;
- materials needed to refresh the research packet.

Useful companion skills: `memo-builder`, `long-short-pitch`, `comps-valuation`, `dcf-model-builder`, `equity-model-update`, `earnings-preview`, `earnings-deep-dive`, `deck-report-qc`, `style-guide-adapter`.

## 3. Investor, client, or counterparty meeting
Objective: help the user understand mandate fit, thesis concerns, evidence gaps, and what would move an investor, client, or counterparty toward action.

Must know:
- counterparty mandate, sector focus, liquidity needs, risk appetite, valuation sensitivity, and prior behavior;
- why this opportunity fits or does not fit;
- likely research objections and decision process.

Question focus:
- investment mandate and non-negotiables;
- valuation, growth, margin, balance sheet, catalyst, and risk assumptions;
- evidence priorities and timeline;
- committee or PM process and gating concerns;
- reasons they might pass or size smaller.

Useful companion skills: `company-tearsheet`, `memo-builder`, `long-short-pitch`, `comps-valuation`, `dcf-model-builder`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `portfolio-risk-management`, `thesis-tracker`.

## 4. Credit, distressed, or risk committee meeting
Objective: pressure-test public issuer credit quality, downside protection, capital structure, liquidity, recovery, and risk limits.

Must know:
- capital structure, debt terms, maturity wall, collateral if disclosed, covenant package, liquidity, cash burn, forecast, and issuer case;
- EBITDA adjustments, non-GAAP definitions, capex, cash taxes, refinancing risk, recovery downside, and trading levels;
- covenant headroom when public or user-provided, rating trajectory, and instrument priority.

Question focus:
- what protects creditors or security holders in the downside;
- covenant cushion if disclosed or user-provided;
- liquidity runway and revolver availability;
- non-GAAP adjustment support and recurring EBITDA;
- collateral and recovery value;
- required monitoring signals.

Useful companion skills: `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `event-driven-analyzer`, `portfolio-risk-management`, `equity-model-update`, `model-audit-tieout`, `memo-builder`. Use Credit Markets for credit instruments, creditworthiness, restructuring, distressed, recovery, spreads, yields, covenants, and debt security analysis.

## 5. Investment committee or internal research review
Objective: prepare the user to make or defend a recommendation, expose the decision needed, and resolve the highest-impact open questions.

Must know:
- thesis, variant view, key risks, valuation, downside, catalysts, evidence status, kill criteria;
- what has changed since last review;
- what the committee must decide today.

Question focus:
- what has to be true to win;
- biggest disconfirming evidence;
- downside case and breakeven points;
- valuation sensitivity and exit assumptions;
- evidence gaps and who owns them;
- go/no-go recommendation.

Useful companion skills: `memo-builder`, `long-short-pitch`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `thesis-tracker`, `portfolio-risk-management`, `deck-report-qc`.

## 6. Earnings, investor day, or public-equity meeting
Objective: prepare for a public-company event, management meeting, sell-side conference, or post-print investor discussion.

Must know:
- latest print, guidance, consensus, KPIs, transcript commentary, estimate revisions, valuation, debate points, catalysts;
- what the market expected versus what changed;
- what question would test the variant view.

Question focus:
- guidance bridge and key drivers;
- demand, pricing, volume, mix, margin, capex, cash flow, buybacks, balance sheet;
- KPI definitions and comparability;
- competitive dynamics and channel signals;
- risks management is not emphasizing;
- what would cause estimates to move.

Live-use output:
- distinguish three or four meeting questions from the fuller earnings research agenda;
- tie each priority question to the expected estimate, valuation, thesis, or monitoring consequence;
- show the follow-up to use if management restates prepared commentary without addressing the investor debate.

Useful companion skills: `earnings-preview`, `earnings-deep-dive`, `equity-model-update`, `long-short-pitch`, `thesis-tracker`, `portfolio-risk-management`, `sector-context-overlay`.

## 7. Expert call
Objective: turn limited expert time into specific evidence on market structure, competitive position, customer behavior, operational reality, or industry change.

Must know:
- expert background and potential bias;
- hypotheses to test;
- what evidence is observable by the expert versus outside their scope;
- compliance restrictions and topics to avoid.

Question focus:
- observable facts and examples rather than opinions;
- triangulation against company claims;
- customer behavior, switching costs, procurement, pricing, sales cycles, churn, channel dynamics;
- competitor positioning;
- leading indicators and inflection points.

Useful companion skills: `company-tearsheet`, `long-short-pitch`, `idea-generation`, `thesis-tracker`, `financial-source-of-truth`, `sector-context-overlay`.

## 8. Portfolio or watchlist review
Objective: identify what changed in a public-equity position or watchlist name, what actions are needed, and what the PM/research lead should do next.

Must know:
- price move, estimate revisions, earnings/KPI trend, liquidity/credit movement, catalyst path, risk/sizing, hedge status, and thesis drift;
- prior action items and ownership;
- management explanations, source quality, and disconfirming evidence.

Question focus:
- what changed versus thesis and what actually matters;
- add, trim, hold, exit, hedge, or keep on watchlist;
- revised estimate/valuation confidence;
- catalysts, break conditions, and downside triggers;
- source gaps that must be resolved before changing exposure.

Useful companion skills: `thesis-tracker`, `equity-model-update`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `portfolio-risk-management`, `model-audit-tieout`, `memo-builder`.

## 9. Forecast, model, valuation, or research review
Objective: prepare the user for decisions on forecast changes, model assumptions, valuation, financial performance, or public-equity-investing research communication.

Must know:
- current model, actuals, forecast, variance drivers, consensus, guidance, targets, and model version;
- what changed since the prior forecast, model update, or research note;
- what decisions are needed and by when.

Question focus:
- source of variance and controllability;
- recurring versus one-time impact;
- forecast tradeoffs, revenue/margin/FCF drivers, capital allocation, valuation, and risk controls;
- forecast confidence and downside plan;
- decisions, owner, and escalation path.

Useful companion skills: `financials-normalizer`, `excel-data-cleaner`, `model-audit-tieout`, `equity-model-update`, `three-statement-model-builder`, `dcf-model-builder`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `memo-builder`, `deck-report-qc`.

## 10. Research kickoff or evidence-request planning
Objective: align on research responsibilities, gating questions, timeline, workstreams, and evidence needed.

Must know:
- research question, coverage universe, deadlines, source package, required model work, open issues;
- who owns each workstream;
- next milestone and decision gate.

Question focus:
- timeline and deliverables;
- owners and dependencies;
- top disconfirming evidence and workstream priorities;
- source, model, and data gaps;
- approval or PM review process;
- communication cadence.

Useful companion skills: `financial-source-of-truth`, `company-tearsheet`, `financials-normalizer`, `equity-model-update`, `comps-valuation`, `dcf-model-builder`, `scenario-sensitivity-generator`, `memo-builder`, `thesis-tracker`.
