# Evidence Hierarchy for Public Equity Investing

Use the hierarchy that fits the claim. The best source is the source closest to the claim, not always the newest or longest document.

## General ranking rules

1. Prefer primary sources over secondary summaries.
2. Prefer filed, audited, executed, court-filed, regulator-published, or system-of-record sources over presentations, emails, and verbal claims.
3. Prefer current-period sources over older sources unless the user requests historical analysis.
4. Prefer source-specific documents over broad marketing materials. For covenant terms requested as credit work, route to Credit Markets. For actual revenue, use filings or earnings releases. For management claims, use the transcript, investor presentation, or meeting note and label it as a claim.
5. Do not rely on a lower-tier source when a higher-tier source is available and directly addresses the claim.
6. When the highest-tier source is unavailable, use lower-tier sources but label the evidence posture.

## Public-company equity and valuation work

Use this for comps, DCF, three-statement models, earnings, initiating coverage, investment memos, public-company model updates, long/short pitches, and economic-impact reports.

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Filed annual reports and audited financial statements: 10-K, 20-F, annual report, audited notes | Historical audited financials, segment data, risks, accounting policies, debt notes | Best source for annual historical financials; may be stale for current events |
| 1 | Filed current or quarterly reports: 10-Q, 6-K, 8-K, prospectus, S-1, F-1, proxy, merger proxy | Quarterly financials, current events, acquisitions, management changes, securities issuance, governance | Use current-event filings for event-specific facts |
| 2 | Earnings release, supplemental, or company-posted investor presentation | KPI detail, non-GAAP reconciliations, management framing, guidance | Company controlled; reconcile with filings when filings are available |
| 2 | Earnings call transcript, investor day transcript, or company-hosted call | Management quotes, guidance explanation, Q&A, qualitative claims | Quote carefully; distinguish statement from your interpretation |
| 3 | Reliable market data or financial data vendor | Price, volume, consensus, estimates, multiples, financial data pulls | Always include vendor and as-of timestamp/date |
| 3 | Sell-side research or third-party expert reports | Consensus debate, variant views, market narrative | Secondary source; do not treat as proof of company facts |
| 4 | News media, blogs, social, unsourced web summaries | Event awareness, sentiment, early signals | Use only with caveats and corroborate before using in decisions |
| 5 | User-provided unsourced assumptions | Scenarios, placeholders, what-if analysis | Label as assumption and show sensitivity |

### EPS quality source order

Use this when an output discusses EPS surprise, operating EPS, recurring EPS, or an ex-gain/ex-loss bridge.

1. Filed statements and notes for GAAP EPS, net income, tax rate, share count, and below-the-line items.
2. Earnings release or filed exhibit for adjusted EPS and company non-GAAP reconciliation.
3. Supplemental deck or prepared remarks for management explanation of one-time or recurring items.
4. Transcript for qualitative explanation and Q&A pushback only; do not use it as the primary source for filed EPS.
5. Consensus/provider export for estimate basis and as-of timestamp.
6. Analyst-derived bridge only when the formula, source inputs, confidence, and remaining gaps are shown.


## Public-equity market, ownership, ETF/index, borrow, and options data

Use this for listed-equity prices, market cap, float, ownership, ETF/index exposure, borrow, options, factor exposure, and liquidity evidence.

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Exchange, issuer, transfer agent, index provider, ETF sponsor, official options/short-interest source, or user-provided risk/portfolio export | share count, float, index membership, ETF holdings, benchmark weight, borrow/short-interest support when available, option-chain source, portfolio exposure | Capture as-of timestamp and methodology; do not infer access if source is unavailable |
| 2 | Callable market-data / consensus / risk-model connector or user-provided vendor export | price, volume, ADV, market cap, EV, consensus, ownership, estimates, factor exposure, borrow, options | Use only if available in runtime or supplied as export; cite provider and timestamp |
| 3 | Broker/sell-side research, expert notes, or third-party reports | debate framing, estimate rationale, positioning color, source leads | Treat as opinion or secondary support, not proof of issuer facts |
| 4 | Public web or news fallback | event awareness or preliminary checks | Label as fallback and request higher-quality export when decision-critical |

## Credit Markets handoff / equity-risk signal sources

Use this section only to source equity-risk signals such as refinancing pressure, maturity walls, liquidity stress, ratings pressure, CDS/spread warning signals, or solvency pressure. If the requested output is Credit Markets research, capital-structure analysis, distressed-security work, covenant review, recovery analysis, or credit committee support, route to Credit Markets.

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Executed credit agreement, indenture, note purchase agreement, intercreditor agreement, security agreement | Equity-risk context for maturity, liquidity, or covenant pressure | Route covenant math and debt-security analysis to Credit Markets |
| 1 | Filed financial statements, debt notes, liquidity disclosures, exchange offers, tender documents, prospectus supplements | Debt balances, maturities, liquidity, issuance, exchange economics, securities terms | Tie debt values to period and filing |
| 1 | Court filings, plan documents, disclosure statements, DIP orders, cash collateral orders | Equity-risk context for restructuring timeline or solvency pressure | Route recovery waterfall and claim-priority analysis to Credit Markets |
| 2 | Rating agency reports, trustee notices, creditor presentations if public or user-provided | Ratings pressure, liquidity context, refinancing risk as equity evidence | Secondary; verify legal terms in Credit Markets when credit work is requested |
| 3 | Callable market-data source or user-provided vendor export | CDS/spread/yield warning signal and issuance context for equity downside | Timestamp market data; do not imply vendor access if unavailable |
| 4 | News, broker notes, creditor presentations, docket summaries | Early warning signals, stakeholder arguments, market sentiment | Corroborate before underwriting or trading conclusion |

## Macro, rates, FX, and commodities as public-equity context

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Central bank releases, government statistical agencies, treasury/finance ministry data, regulator releases | Policy rates, inflation, labor data, GDP, auction results, debt issuance, official reserves | Record release time, revision status, and period |
| 1 | Exchange, clearinghouse, index provider, issuer, or official curve source | Index levels, official rates, settlement data, contract specs relevant to equity analysis | Use exact identifiers when possible; route bond-term work to Credit Markets |
| 2 | Market data vendor or terminal pull | Prices, yields, curves, spreads, vol, FX, commodities | Timestamp and define units/conventions |
| 3 | Bank strategy research, economist notes, market commentary | Interpretation, scenarios, positioning | Treat as opinion, not official data |
| 4 | News and social media | Event detection, color, early reactions | Confirm with primary or market data before conclusion |

## Public REITs and listed real assets

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | REIT filings, audited statements, supplemental packages, investor presentations, property-level disclosures | NOI, same-store NOI, occupancy, AFFO/FFO, leverage, maturity schedule, capex, tenant exposure | Reconcile supplements to filings where available |
| 1 | Public debt documents, prospectuses, rating reports, secured debt disclosures | Equity-risk context for leverage, maturities, liquidity, and solvency risk | Route legal term, covenant, and recovery work to Credit Markets |
| 2 | Market data vendors, public records, comparable sale/lease data | Cap rates, rent comps, supply pipeline, NAV assumptions, local market context | Capture geography, asset type, and date |
| 3 | Broker research, industry publications, news | Market color, rent assumptions, transaction context | Use only as context unless corroborated |

## Event-driven and special situations

| Tier | Source type | Best used for | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Merger agreement, proxy, tender offer document, court/regulator filing, company press release | Definitive terms, consideration, timing, conditions, break fees, regulatory process | Use documents over press summaries |
| 1 | Antitrust/court/agency records, docket filings, exchange notices | Milestones, approvals, hearings, remedies, index/event mechanics | Capture filing date and proceeding status |
| 2 | Company presentations, management calls, investor day materials | Management framing, synergy targets, strategic rationale | Label as company claim unless independently supported |
| 3 | News, sell-side research, event-data providers | Probability framing, market expectations, timeline color | Cite date and avoid treating as definitive |

## Source override rule

If a lower-tier source is more directly relevant to the exact claim than a higher-tier source, explain the override. Example: an investor presentation may be a better source for management's stated long-term target, while the 10-K is a better source for historical audited revenue.

## Unsupported materiality rule

If a claim affects valuation, recommendation, risk sizing, common-equity risk view, or investment action and lacks adequate support, do not bury it in prose. Surface it as an evidence gap or evidence request.
