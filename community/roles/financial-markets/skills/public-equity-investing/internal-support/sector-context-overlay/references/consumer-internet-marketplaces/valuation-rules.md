# Valuation Rules

Deferred reference for `sector-context-overlay` consumer-internet-marketplaces sector lens. Load only when the task needs valuation, public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff, or Credit Markets handoff rules.

## Use marketplace valuation, not generic internet shortcuts

## Equity valuation hierarchy

1. EV / next-twelve-month revenue for high-growth platforms where monetization is still deepening and margins are not yet mature.
2. EV / next-twelve-month gross profit when pass-through costs, payment costs, or logistics costs make revenue less comparable.
3. EV / contribution profit for managed marketplaces where contribution margin best captures local density and unit-economics improvement.
4. EV / EBITDA or FCF yield for more mature, scaled platforms with clearer cash-generation quality.
5. Sum-of-the-parts for hybrids with distinct marketplace, ads, payments, fintech, logistics, or first-party retail components.

### Valuation rules
- Higher multiples belong to dense, habit-forming platforms with strong repeat behavior, improving take-rate quality, high direct traffic, and a credible path to cash generation.
- Penalize models with high multi-homing, incentive dependence, weak cohort retention, heavy logistics intensity, or regulatory overhang.
- Avoid mechanical EV / GMV comparisons across different models. GMV is useful as a scale and liquidity metric, not as a universal valuation base.
- Use gross profit or contribution-profit lenses when revenue contains meaningful pass-through elements.
- Treat ad and fintech layers as valuable only after confirming they do not mask deterioration in core transaction liquidity.

## Credit valuation hierarchy

1. Downside-stable contribution profit and normalized free cash flow.
2. Working-capital resilience if GMV growth slows or reverses.
3. Legal, regulatory, and labor exposure.
4. Liquidity, debt maturity profile, and access to capital.
5. Recovery value of the platform, brand, and any regulated or cash-generative subsidiaries.
