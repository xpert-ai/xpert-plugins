# KPI Cheat Sheet

Deferred reference for `Insurance Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Insurance KPI Map

## 1) Liability promise, business mix, and unit economics
### Metrics to track
- gross written premium, net written premium, net earned premium, or insurance revenue
- retention ratio and ceded ratio
- renewal retention and rate change
- policies in force, account values, deposits, or premiums by product
- short-tail versus long-tail mix
- catastrophe-exposed values or geographic exposure where relevant
- product mix by line, duration, and distribution channel

## 2) Underwriting or liability-margin quality
### Metrics to track
- combined ratio, loss ratio, and expense ratio
- current accident-year combined ratio ex-cat and ex-prior-year development
- catastrophe and large-loss load
- renewal retention and rate change
- new-business margin, spread, or fee mix where disclosed
- net investment spread, base yield, cost of funds, or credited rate
- lapses, surrenders, mortality, morbidity, and disability incidence
- underwriting income, adjusted operating income, and core EPS

## 3) Reserve adequacy and adverse-development risk
### Metrics to track
- prior-year reserve development in dollars and points of combined ratio
- carried reserve to paid and incurred indications if available
- IBNR as a share of reserves
- case reserve adequacy and claim closure trends
- reserve leverage to surplus or equity
- adverse development history by line and accident year
- ceded reserve recoverables and collectability

## 4) Reinsurance, retrocession, and tail-risk transfer
### Metrics to track
- gross versus net written and earned premium
- ceded ratio and retention ratio
- catastrophe PML relative to earnings and capital
- attachment points, reinstatement premiums, and aggregate cover terms
- reserve cover or ADC size where relevant
- recoverables by counterparty and collateral type
- funds-withheld or modco balances

## 5) Investment portfolio and asset-liability management
### Metrics to track
- net investment income and base yield
- new-money rate or reinvestment yield
- variable investment income or alternative-investment contribution
- cost of funds, credited rate, or net investment spread
- fixed income quality mix and structured-product share
- commercial real estate, private credit, CLO, and alternative allocations
- duration gap and key-rate sensitivity
- unrealized losses, AOCI, and derivative collateral exposure

## 6) Capital, solvency, ratings, and holding-company liquidity
### Metrics to track
- RBC or local solvency ratio
- total adjusted capital, statutory surplus, or eligible own funds
- rating-agency capital buffer if disclosed
- financial leverage and fixed-charge coverage
- holdco cash and liquid assets
- statutory dividends upstreamed and upstream capacity
- PML or stress loss relative to capital
- share repurchases, ordinary dividends, and special capital actions

## 7) Earnings quality, accounting, and normalization
### Metrics to track
- normalized ROE or ROA
- underwriting income and ex-cat or ex-PYD underwriting margin
- core spread income and fee income
- contractual service margin and insurance service result where relevant
- adjusted book value, tangible book, or statutory capital growth
- cash remittances and capital-return capacity
