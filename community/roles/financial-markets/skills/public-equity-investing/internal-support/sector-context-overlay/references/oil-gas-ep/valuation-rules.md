# Valuation Rules

Deferred reference for `sector-context-overlay` oil-gas-ep sector lens. Load only when the task needs valuation, public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff, or Credit Markets handoff rules.

## Use E&P-specific valuation, not generic corporate valuation

## Equity valuation hierarchy

### Large-cap or diversified independents
Use these lenses first:
1. NAV based on PDP value plus risk-adjusted undeveloped inventory
2. EV / EBITDAX or EV / cash flow under a clearly stated price deck
3. free-cash-flow yield under strip and under a normalized mid-cycle deck
4. EV / proved reserves and EV / flowing production only as secondary cross-checks

### Shale producers with visible short-cycle inventory
Use these lenses first:
1. NAV with explicit inventory tiering and maintenance-capital logic
2. free-cash-flow yield and cash return potential
3. EV / EBITDAX or EV / DACF adjusted for hedges and differentials
4. value per high-quality location or inventory year only as a sense-check, never a primary method

### Gas-weighted producers
Use these lenses first:
1. NAV under explicit Henry Hub plus basis and transport assumptions
2. free-cash-flow yield at strip and downside winter or shoulder-season cases
3. EV / cash flow after normalizing for basis, transport, and hedge effects
4. optionality value only after proving solvency and transport adequacy

### Offshore, international, or pre-development stories
Use these lenses first:
1. risked NAV or project-level NPV
2. funding-gap and dilution analysis
3. downside liquidity runway and contingent-value framing

### Valuation rules
- Compare only within similar commodity mix, decline profile, inventory depth, balance sheet, and transport burden.
- A low EV / cash-flow multiple can be deserved if the company has short inventory life, poor realized pricing, heavy transport commitments, or weak reserve quality.
- A premium multiple can be deserved if the company has deep tier-one inventory, strong realized pricing, low maintenance capital, and superior capital-allocation discipline.
- Never use EV / flowing barrel or EV / BOE of reserves by itself.
- Do not capitalize one quarter of unusually strong commodity prices.
- Do not use PV-10 or standardized measure mechanically without adjusting for taxes, hedges, undeveloped timing, and the difference between SEC pricing and investor price expectations.

## Debt valuation hierarchy
- PDP reserve value and downside reserve value
- claim priority and collateral position
- liquidity runway and maturity wall
- hedging, transport, and fixed-charge burden
- reserve revision risk and borrowing-base sensitivity
- abandonment and environmental liability burden
