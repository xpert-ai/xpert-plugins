# KPI Cheat Sheet

Deferred reference for `Exchanges Market Infrastructure Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Exchange and market infrastructure KPI cheat sheet

Use these terms precisely and only after checking company definitions.

- ADV: average daily volume. Usually contracts, shares, or trades. Always confirm whether the company reports contracts, notional, or shares.
- ADNV: average daily notional value. Common in repo, FX, fixed income, and cash equities.
- Open interest: end-period outstanding derivatives contracts. Use it to judge franchise stickiness and position inventory, not just trading heat.
- Average rate per contract or RPC: clearing and transaction fees divided by contracts traded. Use only within the company’s own definition.
- Net capture: net revenue earned per share or per notional traded after rebates or direct trading costs. Gross volume with weak capture can be low quality.
- FPM: fees per million notional traded. Common in electronic fixed income, rates, FX, repo, and other OTC or RFQ-style venues.
- Liquidity payments or rebates: payments to market makers or order-flow providers that reduce net revenue quality.
- Recurring revenue: data, connectivity, index licensing, annual listings, workflow, technology, and subscription-like revenue. Verify contract structure and renewal quality.
- ARR: annualized recurring revenue. Useful for recurring businesses, but definitions vary and are not safely comparable across companies without adjustment.
- Initial margin / performance bond: collateral posted to cover potential future exposure at a CCP.
- Variation margin: mark-to-market cash exchanged as positions move.
- Default fund: mutualized resources contributed by clearing members and sometimes supplemented by CCP capital.
- Eligible collateral: the assets a clearinghouse will accept. This matters for customer behavior, funding efficiency, and CCP attractiveness.
- Market share: only use after checking the denominator. Shares based on company-defined TRACE, matched shares, or notional can overstate or understate the economic position.
- New-product mix: the share of economics from recently launched contracts, data products, auction protocols, or workflow tools. This is how secular growth often shows up before the P&L fully reflects it.

## Exchange KPI Map

## 1) Business mix, economic model, and regulatory perimeter
### Metrics to track
- revenue by segment and by recurring versus transactional classification
- net revenue versus gross revenue where relevant
- EBITDA or operating income by segment
- percentage of revenue from top products, top geographies, and top business lines
- debt at holdco versus regulated subsidiary level

## 2) Volume quality, product mix, and franchise durability
### Metrics to track
- ADV or ADNV by product and asset class
- open interest or outstanding positions where relevant
- benchmark-franchise concentration
- volume by customer channel or protocol where disclosed
- share of activity from proprietary products versus fungible products

## 3) Monetization: rpc, net capture, fpm, and fee quality
### Metrics to track
- average rate per contract or RPC
- net capture per share or per notional
- FPM by asset class or protocol
- liquidity payments and rebate intensity
- royalty or licensing fees as a percentage of product revenue

## 4) Recurring revenue: data, connectivity, index, listings, and workflow
### Metrics to track
- recurring revenue as a percentage of total revenue
- ARR or annualized subscription metrics where defined
- data and connectivity growth
- index-linked assets or benchmark-linked product growth where disclosed
- annual listings or issuer-services revenue versus transaction listings revenue

## 5) Clearing, collateral, rate sensitivity, and balance economics
### Metrics to track
- initial margin and variation-margin flows where disclosed
- default fund size and contribution framework
- cash and non-cash collateral balances
- interest income or spread earned on margin-related balances
- member concentration and client-clearing penetration

## 6) Competitive position, market structure, and switching costs
### Metrics to track
- market share by product and region
- customer count or member count where disclosed
- top-product concentration
- share of revenue from proprietary or benchmark products
- connectivity, subscription, or workflow attachment metrics where disclosed

## 7) Technology, resilience, cyber, and operating leverage
### Metrics to track
- adjusted operating margin and incremental margin
- technology and capex spend
- capitalized software as a share of development spend where disclosed
- incident history, fines, or remediation costs
- employee mix in technology and product if discussed

## 8) Growth drivers: new products, secular electronification, and cross-sell
### Metrics to track
- revenue from new products or new protocols where disclosed
- adoption metrics by channel or asset class
- organic revenue growth versus acquisition contribution
- international growth versus domestic growth
- cross-sell or multi-product client adoption measures where disclosed

## 9) Capital allocation, balance sheet, and public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff
### Metrics to track
- gross and net leverage
- debt maturity ladder
- free cash flow conversion
- buyback and dividend payout profile
- holdco cash and subsidiary cash availability

## 10) Guidance quality, normalization risk, and scenario analysis
### Metrics to track
- volume and revenue sensitivity to volatility proxies
- rate sensitivity on margin or customer-cash economics
- recurring revenue growth under lower activity assumptions
- segment-level downside and upside scenarios
- normalized EPS, EBITDA, and free cash flow
