# Red Flags And Failure Modes

Deferred reference for `Oil Gas E&P Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Red flags and common traps

Treat these as high-priority warning signals:
- reserve replacement sustained only by acquisitions rather than organic drilling
- reserve growth driven mainly by price revisions or looser booking assumptions
- high PUD proportion with weak funding visibility or stretched development schedule
- management highlighting location count while avoiding value per location or inventory quality
- production growth that requires materially outspending cash flow with no clear per-share value creation
- steep decline masked by acquisitions or unusually high completion cadence
- strong benchmark prices but weak realized pricing because of basis, transport, or quality issues
- low LOE used as the hero metric while GPT, water, or corporate costs rise elsewhere
- cheap EV / cash-flow multiple on a short-duration or high-obligation asset base
- type curves or EURs inflated by longer laterals, wider spacing assumptions, or immature data sets
- non-operated exposure framed as capital flexibility even though schedule control is weak
- large transport or MVC commitments that become fixed-charge pain in a downturn
- hedges rolling off just as leverage rises or capex commitments remain high
- abandonment liabilities or remediation burden treated as distant rather than real
- M&A presented as inventory extension without clear cost-of-acquired-inventory math
- one commodity or one basin concentration ignored because BOE reporting looks diversified
## Never do this

- Do not lead with revenue growth, EPS growth, or generic EBITDA margin.
- Do not compare producers on blended BOE metrics without decomposing commodity mix.
- Do not accept reserve replacement without identifying the source of the additions.
- Do not annualize favorable spot prices or hedge gains.
- Do not use EV / flowing barrel or EV / proved reserves as standalone valuation methods.
- Do not underwrite a gas producer on Henry Hub without basis and transport analysis.
- Do not confuse lower capex with lower maintenance capital unless decline has been proven.
- Do not treat PUD value as equivalent to PDP value.
- Do not assume acreage count equals inventory quality.
- Do not underwrite credit without downside reserve value and liquidity analysis.
