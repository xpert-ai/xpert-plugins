# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `REIT Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
Focus on what the market is mispricing about same-store growth durability, mark-to-market rent, capex burden, balance-sheet flexibility, or private-market NAV.

## Event-driven
Prioritize catalysts: asset sales, take-private or activist setups, capital raises, dividend changes, refinancing events, tenant bankruptcies, large redevelopments, spin-offs, portfolio simplification, rating actions, and M&A.

## Macro
Emphasize rates, cap rates, financing spreads, construction costs, supply delivery, labor and insurance inflation, consumer demand, business formation, and property-sector cycle position.

## Quant / systematic
Standardize the fastest-moving and most predictive variables: same-store NOI, occupancy, leasing spreads, rent coverage, retained AFFO, leverage, maturity wall, and valuation versus NAV or historical multiples.

## Credit Markets handoff / equity-risk debt context
Focus on collateral quality, unsecured versus secured claim position, unencumbered pool, refinancing risk, covenant headroom, tenant stress, and recovery under downside cap-rate and occupancy assumptions.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
Translate growth into per-share AFFO and NAV growth, not just gross asset growth. Favor REITs with real internal growth, disciplined development, and capital access that does not depend on serial issuance at fragile valuations.
## Output overlays when this skill is used with common finance tasks

## With an earnings deep dive
Always add:
- same-store NOI bridge
- leasing and occupancy dashboard
- rent-roll or rollover summary
- balance-sheet and maturity-ladder table
- AFFO and dividend-quality bridge
- next 12 to 24 month catalysts by sector cycle and financing need

## With an investment memo
Always include:
- REIT archetype and sector-cycle position
- core thesis by assets, leases, balance sheet, valuation, and catalyst
- variant perception
- private-market NAV view and public-equity multiple view
- disconfirming evidence
- security-specific recommendation with stack position and downside case

## With a financial model
Always produce:
- same-store revenue, expense, and NOI schedules
- acquisition, disposition, and development schedules
- recurring capex and TI/LC schedules
- debt and interest schedule with maturity assumptions
- FFO, AFFO, and dividend outputs
- NAV and cap-rate sensitivities

## When debt or structured credit work is requested, route to Credit Markets
Always include:
- unencumbered-asset and unsecured-debt coverage discussion
- maturity or refinancing path
- NOI and asset-value downside stress
- recovery discussion tied to claim position and collateral leakage

Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.

## Default writing style

Write like a veteran REIT analyst:
- lead with the property cash-flow driver, not the accounting metric
- separate same-store from external growth
- separate contractual rent from true cash economics
- quantify recurring cash leakage between NOI and AFFO
- tie valuation to asset quality, balance sheet, and cycle position
- be skeptical of "accretion," "stability," and "yield" language unless proven with cash flow and asset values

When unsure, prioritize rent-roll quality, balance-sheet resilience, recurring capex burden, and downside NAV over headline dividend yield or one quarter of FFO upside.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
