# Archetypes And Classification

Deferred reference for `SaaS Subscription Software Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## First classify the software company before analyzing it

Classify the company first. Do not compare all recurring-software businesses with one template.

## Archetypes

### Horizontal enterprise workflow / system-of-record suite
- Core engines: mission-critical workflows, seat growth, edition upgrades, module attach, price realization, and platform consolidation.
- Primary watch items: seat expansion, cross-sell, enterprise standardization, renewal quality, service dependency, and competitive bundling.
- Common trap: treating revenue growth as purely new-logo driven when most value is actually in multi-product expansion inside the base.

### Vertical SaaS
- Core engines: deep domain workflow fit, low churn from embedded operations, pricing power through mission-critical functionality, and workflow expansion.
- Primary watch items: end-market health, customer concentration by vertical, implementation complexity, payments or fintech attach, and whether the company is truly software or partially labor-enabled services.
- Common trap: overpaying for low churn without testing whether growth is capped by the vertical’s size or by weak upsell paths.

### Dev tools / collaboration / seat-based platform
- Core engines: user growth, paid-seat conversion, enterprise expansion, adjacent product attach, community or ecosystem pull, and workflow standardization.
- Primary watch items: seat optimization, free-to-paid conversion, cohort maturity, AI seat attach, open-source substitution, and cross-functional expansion beyond technical users.
- Common trap: assuming seat growth is durable without testing whether a prior hiring boom or migration wave pulled demand forward.

### Cybersecurity / observability / infrastructure software with usage or hybrid pricing
- Core engines: workload growth, data-ingestion growth, node or asset expansion, module attach, security incidents that drive urgency, and pricing architecture.
- Primary watch items: committed versus overage revenue, cloud cost burden, customer optimization, multi-product penetration, and how elastic usage is under macro pressure.
- Common trap: reading a strong quarter as structural acceleration when revenue was heavily influenced by bursty consumption or a one-time incident environment.

### Data platform / cloud platform with consumption pricing
- Core engines: customer workloads, storage and compute consumption, committed capacity, overages, platform breadth, and data/AI workload growth.
- Primary watch items: visibility versus volatility, RPO quality, workload concentration, large-customer behavior, and whether revenue is governed by consumption timing rather than contract size.
- Common trap: applying classic seat-based SaaS frameworks to a model where product revenue depends on usage behavior rather than straight-line subscription recognition.

### Hybrid seat plus usage platform
- Core engines: stable seat base plus elastic usage layers, module expansion, enterprise standardization, and AI or automation usage monetization.
- Primary watch items: which portion of growth is recurring base versus variable workload, whether usage is incremental or cannibalistic, and how blended margins evolve.
- Common trap: using one valuation multiple on a business whose subscription and consumption components deserve different durability assumptions.

### SMB / mid-market PLG platform
- Core engines: self-serve acquisition, trial conversion, expansion within small accounts, ecosystem distribution, and low-friction onboarding.
- Primary watch items: logo churn, ARPU expansion, sales-assisted upmarket motion, CAC inflation, and macro sensitivity of smaller customers.
- Common trap: over-focusing on customer-count growth without testing payback, retention, and whether larger-customer penetration is actually improving.

### Hybrid license / maintenance / subscription transition story
- Core engines: maintenance base durability, cloud migration, subscription mix shift, upsell into cloud products, and margin improvement from delivery-model change.
- Primary watch items: cannibalization, migration pacing, deferred-revenue and RPO comparability, sales-comp redesign, and the real shape of the installed-base conversion funnel.
- Common trap: giving cloud mix credit twice by ignoring that some growth is merely migration of legacy revenue into a new bucket.

### Multi-product roll-up / platform consolidator
- Core engines: acquisition integration, cross-sell, pricing standardization, back-office leverage, and rationalization of overlapping products.
- Primary watch items: organic versus acquired ARR growth, customer overlap, integration execution, churn of acquired cohorts, and whether platform rhetoric is supported by real attach rates.
- Common trap: valuing acquired growth at the same multiple as clean organic platform growth.

### Distressed or reacceleration story
- Core engines: installed-base durability, path to stable growth, operating-cost reset, product relevance, and balance-sheet runway.
- Primary watch items: churn inflection, renewal calendar, covenant headroom, sales-force productivity, leadership credibility, and whether AI or product repositioning is real or promotional.
- Common trap: treating a single quarter of stabilized billings or margin as proof of a durable turnaround.
