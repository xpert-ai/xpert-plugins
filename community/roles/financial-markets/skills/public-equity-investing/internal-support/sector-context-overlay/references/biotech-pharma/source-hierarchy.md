# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `Biotech Pharma Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use sources in this order:

1. 10-K, 10-Q, annual report, earnings release, 8-K top-line data disclosure, and official investor presentation
2. protocol, statistical analysis plan, and ClinicalTrials.gov record when available
3. peer-reviewed papers, major medical-meeting abstracts and presentations, and KOL-relevant scientific disclosures
4. FDA label, approval letter, briefing documents, AdCom materials, inspection or manufacturing disclosures when public, and other official regulator documents
5. Orange Book, Purple Book, patent-litigation or settlement disclosures, and exclusivity references
6. management commentary on pricing, reimbursement, channel inventory, patient-finding, and manufacturing
7. peer disclosures for market-share, pricing, endpoint, and label comparability checks

## Reconciliation rules
- Protocol and regulator-facing documents outrank slide-deck spin.
- Pre-specified endpoints outrank post-hoc analyses.
- Net sales outrank script anecdotes when gross-to-net is moving.
- Script data, patient-start data, and inventory data must be reconciled before concluding that demand accelerated.
- Trial comparisons across companies are only valid after aligning population, line of therapy, endpoint definition, follow-up time, control-arm strength, and geography.
- If management stops disclosing a launch or patient metric, treat the absence as information.
- If the company mixes product sales with milestones, royalties, and collaboration revenue, analyze each separately before recombining.
## Biotech- and pharma-specific diligence requests

Request these when deeper underwriting is needed:

- patient-flow bridge from prevalence to treated patients by geography and line of therapy
- protocol, endpoint hierarchy, powering assumptions, multiplicity plan, stratification, and interim-analysis rules
- detailed efficacy tables and safety tables including discontinuations and dose modifications
- subgroup and biomarker analysis with clear separation of pre-specified versus exploratory work
- manufacturing map including sites, suppliers, tech transfer, release testing, capacity, and backup plans
- gross-to-net bridge by rebate, chargeback, copay, return, distribution fee, and channel mix
- wholesaler and specialty-pharmacy inventory levels where relevant
- payer-coverage status, coding, reimbursement timing, and prior-authorization friction
- exclusivity and patent timeline with litigation status and likely entrant timing
- collaboration economics including milestones, royalties, opt-ins, co-funding, and geographic splits
- runway analysis under base, delayed-catalyst, and failed-readout cases
- convert terms, royalty-financing terms, lien package, and covenant definitions for credit work
- launch cohort data: patient starts, persistency, refill behavior, and prescriber breadth
