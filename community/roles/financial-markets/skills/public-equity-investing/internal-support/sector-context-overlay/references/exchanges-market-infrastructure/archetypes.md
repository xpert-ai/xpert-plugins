# Archetypes And Classification

Deferred reference for `Exchanges Market Infrastructure Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## First classify the company

Classify the company before analyzing it. Do not compare all exchange and market-infrastructure businesses mechanically.

## Archetypes

### Listed derivatives exchange plus central counterparty
- Core engines: benchmark contract ADV, open interest, product mix, clearing economics, margin efficiencies, and market-data monetization.
- Primary watch items: benchmark-product concentration, rate or commodity volatility sensitivity, average rate per contract, collateral balances, member concentration, regulatory changes, and cross-margin value.
- Common trap: extrapolating crisis-period activity without testing whether higher volume came from durable hedging adoption or temporary panic.

### Cash equities and options exchange network
- Core engines: matched-share volume, options volume, proprietary products, market share, connectivity, access fees, and data.
- Primary watch items: rebates and liquidity payments, proprietary-index options mix, market-center share shifts, exchange-routing dynamics, and regulatory pressure on fees or market structure.
- Common trap: focusing on gross revenue or share volume without measuring net capture after rebates and pass-through items.

### Diversified exchange, index, data, listings, and workflow conglomerate
- Core engines: transaction revenue in markets businesses plus recurring revenue from data, indices, listings, software, and workflow tools.
- Primary watch items: recurring versus transactional mix, cross-sell, acquisition integration, debt load, data pricing power, capital-markets sensitivity in listings, and software retention.
- Common trap: valuing the entire company on one exchange multiple or one software multiple instead of separating the transaction business from the recurring franchise.

### Electronic fixed-income, rates, FX, or repo venue
- Core engines: ADNV or ADV, protocol mix, market share, fixed fees, variable fees, automation, and client workflow integration.
- Primary watch items: FPM, protocol shifts, all-to-all adoption, portfolio-trading mix, compression, international growth, and product-channel mix.
- Common trap: assuming all volume growth is equally valuable when the fastest-growing protocols may earn lower fees per million.

### Clearing, post-trade, and collateral utility
- Core engines: cleared notional, member and client penetration, margin balances, eligible-collateral breadth, netting benefits, and settlement or post-trade workflow revenue.
- Primary watch items: default waterfall design, resource sufficiency, concentration of clearing members, collateral policy, regulatory capital treatment, and operational resilience.
- Common trap: treating the business like a pure software or exchange franchise and underweighting systemic-risk, legal, and operational-continuity issues.

### Listings, index, and market-data franchise
- Core engines: annual issuer fees, index licensing, benchmark use, assets linked to products, pricing power, and subscription renewals.
- Primary watch items: AUM and net-flow sensitivity, benchmark competition, client concentration, data redistribution pricing, and capital-markets issuance cycles.
- Common trap: labeling it fully cyclical like an exchange or fully recurring like software without decomposing the real exposure.

### Hybrid company with multiple archetypes
If the company spans more than one archetype, split it before concluding anything. A hybrid business with a strong recurring-data segment can deserve a different valuation and downside case than its more cyclical transaction segment would suggest on its own.
