# Valuation Rules

Deferred reference for `sector-context-overlay` bank sector lens. Load only when the task needs valuation, public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff, or Credit Markets handoff rules.

## Use bank-specific valuation, not generic corporate valuation

## Equity valuation hierarchy
Use these lenses first:
1. price / tangible book value
2. price / earnings on normalized EPS
3. ROTCE relative to cost of equity
4. excess return / residual income
5. dividend discount where the franchise is mature and payout is meaningful

### Valuation rules
- A higher P/TBV is justified by sustainable excess ROTCE, funding quality, fee durability, capital optionality, and lower tail risk.
- Two banks with similar current EPS can deserve very different multiples if one is over-earning on rates or under-reserving on credit.
- Tangible book matters most when capital is real and not flattered by accounting or hidden liquidity stress.
- Use sum-of-the-parts only when business lines truly deserve different capital intensity and multiples.

## Debt valuation hierarchy
- spread versus expected loss and recovery
- distance to regulatory minima and intervention triggers
- refinancing path and call incentives
- entity-level structural protection or subordination
- liquidity runway and access to secured or unsecured markets
