# Archetypes And Classification

Deferred reference for `Biotech Pharma Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## First classify the biotech or pharma company before analyzing it

Classify the company first. Do not compare everything in the sector on one multiple or one catalyst framework.

## Archetypes

### Discovery or preclinical platform biotech
- Core engines: target discovery, platform validation, partner interest, preclinical differentiation, option value, and financing access.
- Primary watch items: biological plausibility, translation risk, breadth versus depth, quality of early datasets, partner economics, and burn versus time to human proof of concept.
- Common trap: paying for a platform multiple before any human evidence exists or counting optionality multiple times across similar assets.

### Single-asset or tightly concentrated clinical-stage biotech
- Core engines: one or two readouts, one regulatory path, and one financing clock.
- Primary watch items: protocol quality, endpoint appropriateness, control-arm relevance, data maturity, label breadth, and cash runway through the next catalyst.
- Common trap: treating management confidence or early uncontrolled data as if the asset were already validated.

### Multi-asset clinical-stage biotech
- Core engines: diversified catalyst set, platform validation across assets, capital allocation, and partnership flexibility.
- Primary watch items: whether the assets are truly independent, whether the organization can execute multiple studies, and whether platform value is being supported by human evidence.
- Common trap: describing the company as diversified when multiple programs are exposed to the same scientific bet or manufacturing bottleneck.

### Cell therapy, gene therapy, or other advanced-modality developer
- Core engines: transformative efficacy, durability, manufacturing execution, treatment-center activation, and reimbursement viability.
- Primary watch items: safety, durability of response, comparability, vein-to-vein time or treatment logistics, vector or starting-material supply, and COGS.
- Common trap: modeling clinical efficacy upside without pricing in CMC, capacity, site-of-care, and payer-friction risk.

### Commercial rare-disease or specialty biotech
- Core engines: patient finding, diagnosis, treatment initiation, persistence, label expansion, and premium pricing defended by meaningful benefit.
- Primary watch items: true prevalence, testing funnel, new prescriber growth, refill or compliance trends, gross-to-net, and competitive encroachment.
- Common trap: annualizing early launch demand before testing how much came from pent-up patients, channel loading, or easy-to-find patients.

### Specialty pharma or in-licensed branded-asset portfolio company
- Core engines: business development, lifecycle management, price-volume mix, channel execution, and SG&A leverage.
- Primary watch items: gross-to-net, payer rebates, copay support, inventory, tender timing, formulation or line-extension economics, and debt or royalty burdens.
- Common trap: mistaking price-led growth or acquisition-led growth for durable organic franchise strength.

### Large-cap diversified branded pharma
- Core engines: mature-franchise defense, pipeline replacement, global market access, manufacturing reliability, M&A, and capital allocation.
- Primary watch items: patent cliff timing, new-product ramp, lifecycle management, business-development economics, litigation, tax, and FCF conversion.
- Common trap: valuing near-term earnings too highly while ignoring the post-LOE earnings hole.

### Generic or biosimilar-exposed pharma company
- Core engines: cost position, manufacturing reliability, tender wins, launch cadence, litigation, and working-capital discipline.
- Primary watch items: price erosion, customer concentration, regulatory compliance, plant quality, and mix shift into more defendable products.
- Common trap: using innovative-biopharma valuation logic on a business governed mostly by procurement and cost.

### Royalty, milestone, or structured-life-sciences finance vehicle
- Core engines: diversified contractual cash flows, low operating cost, partner credit quality, and duration of royalty streams.
- Primary watch items: concentration, asset expiry, step-down schedules, underlying product durability, and legal seniority of claims.
- Common trap: treating a high-yielding royalty stream as if it has no patent-cliff or label-risk exposure.
