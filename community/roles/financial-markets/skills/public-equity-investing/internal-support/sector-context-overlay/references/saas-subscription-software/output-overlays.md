# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `SaaS Subscription Software Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
- Focus on retention durability, cohort behavior, cRPO quality, GTM efficiency, multiple durability, and whether AI changes the slope of growth or only the story.

## Event-driven
- Focus on beat-and-raise quality, backlog-to-revenue conversion, CFO or CRO changes, pricing or packaging shifts, activist angles, strategic M&A, convert overhangs, and whether AI announcements translate into paid demand.

## Macro
- Focus on IT-budget sensitivity, seat optimization risk, usage elasticity, enterprise spending cycles, FX, public-cloud cost trends, and hiring conditions that influence seat growth.

## Quant / systematic
- Standardize around revenue growth, cRPO growth, billings or calculated billings where relevant, retention trends, large-customer count growth, gross-margin trend, FCF conversion, dilution, and estimate-revision direction.

## Credit Markets handoff / structured-finance context
- Focus on contract durability, annual-prepay mix, customer concentration, issuer behavior, acquisition appetite, covenant definitions, working-capital support from deferred revenue, liquidity runway, and downside cash generation if growth slows sharply.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
- Focus on installed-base expansion, product adjacency, attach-rate trajectory, platform depth, TAM expansion, AI monetization realism, and whether growth is compounding with acceptable dilution.
## Output overlays for common tasks

## For a post-earnings deep dive
- Lead with the driver tree: new logos, expansion, retention, billings / cRPO, margin, and FCF quality.
- Explicitly separate seat-based and usage-based components.
- Call out any metric-definition changes, duration effects, acquisition effects, or AI-monetization noise.
- Show what changed in the quarter that matters for the next four quarters, not just what beat consensus.
- End with the debate: why bulls think durability improved and why bears think quality weakened.

## For a pre-earnings preview
- Focus on setup around guide, cRPO or billings expectations, deal linearity, consumption trends, hiring and seat trends, and cloud-cost or AI-margin risk.
- Identify which metrics the market is most likely to key on for that name.
- State whether the quarter is more likely to hinge on demand, conversion of backlog, margin, or AI narrative.

## For an investment memo
- Make the core debate about durability of the installed base and the right valuation framework.
- Include a clean bull, base, and bear case around retention, expansion, GTM efficiency, and margin path.
- State clearly whether the moat is workflow, data, ecosystem, switching cost, or distribution.
- Separate real platform expansion from revenue complexity and accounting noise.

## For a financial model
- Build a bottom-up revenue bridge that reflects the company’s actual pricing architecture.
- Link backlog metrics to revenue only in a way consistent with the company’s billing cadence and model.
- Model subscription gross margin, services drag, AI cost burden, and dilution explicitly.
- Include scenario cases for retention, new-logo adds, duration changes, and AI monetization.

## When debt or structured work is requested, route to Credit Markets
- Prioritize renewal durability, customer concentration, liquidity, debt maturities, covenants, and normalized cash generation under slower growth.
- Treat deferred revenue as helpful working capital but not as a substitute for liquidity.
- Evaluate whether the company could keep servicing debt if growth fell sharply and margin expansion stalled.
Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.
## Style rules for using this sector pack

- Write like a veteran buy-side software analyst, not like a generic technology summarizer.
- Always define the company’s own metrics before using them in an argument.
- Separate facts, management framing, and your inference.
- Be explicit about what is contractual visibility and what is behavioral visibility.
- Distinguish seat growth, price, usage, cross-sell, and AI uplift instead of rolling them into one word like expansion.
- When the company is hybrid, present the subscription and consumption pieces separately before combining them.
- Treat software as a gross-profit and cash-flow business, not just a revenue-growth story.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
