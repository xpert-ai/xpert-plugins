# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `Consumer Internet Marketplaces Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use sources in this order:

1. 10-K, 10-Q, annual report, earnings release, and official shareholder letter
2. KPI appendices, investor-day materials, and formal metric definitions
3. revenue-recognition, customer-funds, seller-payable, and legal-reserve footnotes
4. management commentary on pricing, incentives, liquidity, new categories, and regulatory matters
5. trusted third-party app, traffic, transaction, or credit-card data only after reconciling with company definitions
6. peer filings for normalization and definition checks

## Reconciliation rules
- Never compare GMV, GMS, GOV, GBV, or gross bookings across companies until taxes, shipping, tips, refunds, cancellations, and off-platform activity are normalized.
- Never compare active-buyer metrics until the measurement window is normalized.
- Always separate gross versus net revenue recognition before reading relative growth or take rate.
- If a company has ads, payments, fintech, or first-party businesses, analyze those pieces separately before recombining.
- Normalize for FX, leap years, pandemic or post-pandemic distortions, large event timing, and acquisitions or divestitures.
- If management stops disclosing a KPI, treat the absence as information.
- If the business is two-sided locally, prioritize local-density evidence over national aggregates.
## Consumer internet and marketplace-specific diligence requests

Request these when deeper underwriting is needed:

- cohort tables by acquisition month, channel, category, and geography
- repeat-rate and order-frequency curves for mature cohorts
- reactivation behavior and first-year value of reactivated cohorts
- active buyers or consumers, active sellers or merchants, and liquidity metrics by geography and category
- supply retention, time to first transaction, and supply-side incentive history
- conversion, fill rate, cancellation, refund, and defect metrics by category and region
- take-rate bridge by core transaction, ads, subscriptions, payments, shipping, and other services
- paid marketing mix, direct traffic mix, CAC by channel, and payback by cohort
- promoted-listing or ads penetration and merchant ROI evidence
- membership penetration, cross-category behavior, and attach rates to new services
- trust-and-safety metrics including fraud, counterfeit, chargeback, dispute, and insurance-loss trends
- contribution margin by geography or category if available
- customer-funds, seller-payables, and restricted-cash schedules
- legal and regulatory matters by geography, especially labor, travel, tax, privacy, and platform obligations
- debt maturities, convert terms, and any warehouse, receivable, or guarantee obligations
