# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `Biotech Pharma Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
- Focus on the real asset-level debate: evidence quality, probability-weighted label value, launch trajectory, gross-to-net risk, and the shape of the patent cliff versus replacement pipeline.

## Event-driven
- Focus on the catalyst tree: readout setup, endpoint win conditions, AdCom optics, PDUFA risk, CRL repair time, M&A optionality, financing overhang, and what the market already expects.

## Macro
- Focus on drug-pricing policy, reimbursement pressure, biotech financing windows, rates and risk appetite, FDA pace and enforcement tone, M&A appetite, and how those change the sector's cost of capital and multiple structure.

## Quant / systematic
- Standardize around catalyst calendar, estimate revisions, launch trajectory, gross-to-net trends, burn and dilution, balance-sheet strength, volatility around binary events, and LOE exposure for mature pharma.

## Credit Markets handoff / structured-finance context
- Focus on runway to value inflection, collateral quality, royalty encumbrance, covenant tightness, minimum liquidity, debt maturity versus catalyst timing, and downside cash generation if adoption or approval slips.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
- Focus on platform repeatability, number of shots on goal, commercial-infrastructure leverage, ability to extend into adjacent indications, and whether reinvestment is compounding value rather than simply extending runway.
## Output overlays for common tasks

## For a post-earnings deep dive
- Lead with what changed in the asset-level probability tree, not just with EPS or revenue versus consensus.
- Separate product demand, gross-to-net, pipeline progress, manufacturing readiness, and financing runway.
- Call out whether quarter strength came from recurring product economics or from collaboration and milestone noise.
- End with the real debate: what bulls think got de-risked and what bears think remains fragile.

## For a pre-earnings preview
- Focus on the next 1 to 4 catalysts: readouts, filings, AdComs, PDUFAs, launch inflections, financing risk, and LOE-sensitive franchise performance.
- Identify which reported metrics matter most for that name: patient starts, scripts, gross-to-net, inventory, R&D timing, or pure runway.
- State clearly whether the setup is about numbers, catalyst framing, financing, or business-development optionality.

## For an investment memo
- Make the core debate about evidence quality, regulatory and label risk, and commercial durability.
- Use a clear bull, base, and bear case around label, launch, LOE timing, and financing path.
- Show where value sits today: cash, base business, lead asset, follow-on assets, and optionality.
- Be explicit about what must happen next for the thesis to work.

## For a financial model
- Build bottom-up patient-flow and probability-adjusted asset schedules instead of relying on a single revenue CAGR.
- Link catalysts to spend, financing, and valuation changes.
- Model gross-to-net, persistence, capacity, and LOE explicitly.
- Include scenario cases for failed readout, delayed approval, narrow label, weak reimbursement, and faster-than-expected erosion.

## When debt or structured work is requested, route to Credit Markets
- Prioritize liquidity runway, debt or royalty seniority, covenant headroom, and catalyst timing relative to maturities.
- Test downside under readout failure, CRL, delayed launch, reimbursement friction, and erosion of the underlying royalty stream.
- Treat IP value and pipeline optionality cautiously in recovery unless there is a proven commercial product or clearly monetizable royalty asset.
Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.
## Style rules for using this sector pack

- Write like a veteran buy-side biotech or pharma analyst, not like a generic healthcare summarizer.
- Always say what stage the asset is in, what the endpoint was, how many patients were studied, and what the comparison standard is.
- Separate facts, management framing, and your inference.
- Distinguish biological plausibility, statistical significance, regulatory acceptability, and commercial attractiveness instead of blending them together.
- Treat time as a value driver: months of delay matter for runway, dilution, and NPV.
- Distinguish gross sales, net sales, patient starts, persistence, and inventory movement instead of calling everything demand.
- When the company is diversified, present mature franchises, launch assets, and pipeline options separately before combining them.
- Treat the label and the access funnel as part of the asset, not as afterthoughts.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
