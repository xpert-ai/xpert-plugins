# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `Bank Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
Focus on what the market is mispricing about normalized ROTCE, capital return, funding durability, or credit costs. Force a bull/base/bear on NII, credit, and capital.

## Event-driven
Prioritize catalysts: M&A, divestitures, consent orders, stress-test outcomes, capital-return changes, reserve releases/builds, management changes, asset sales, recapitalizations, regulatory settlements.

## Macro
Emphasize rate sensitivity, deposit migration, curve shape, unemployment, property-market stress, sovereign or FX transmission, and policy/regulatory regime changes.

## Quant / systematic
Standardize the variables that revise fastest and matter most: NII guidance changes, deposit cost progression, beta, reserve trajectory, NCO trend, capital buffer, tangible book growth, efficiency ratio trend, and securities-mark pressure.

## Credit Markets handoff / equity-risk debt context
Focus on legal entity, ranking, collateral, encumbrance, resolution dynamics, deposit preference, refinance risk, and downside recovery under regulatory intervention.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
Translate “growth” into capital-efficient profitable balance-sheet expansion, durable low-cost funding, fee-pool share gains, branchless acquisition efficiency, cross-sell density, and technology-enabled operating leverage without weakening controls.
## Output overlays when this skill is used with common finance tasks

## With an earnings deep dive
Always add:
- NII/NIM bridge
- deposit and funding table
- credit scorecard
- capital and payout bridge
- liquidity / securities / AOCI snapshot
- next-quarter watch items and what changed in guidance

## With an investment memo
Always include:
- bank archetype and why the market is likely framing it wrong or right
- core thesis by funding, NII, credit, capital, and catalyst
- variant perception
- disconfirming evidence
- security-specific recommendation with stack position and risk/reward

## With a financial model
Always produce:
- average-balance driver tree
- rate and beta assumptions by portfolio
- credit-cost framework
- RWA and capital schedule
- TBVPS and ROTCE output
- explicit rate, credit, and capital sensitivities

## When debt or structured credit work is requested, route to Credit Markets
Always include:
- legal entity and structural subordination map
- recovery and encumbrance assessment
- liquidity runway versus near-term uses
- regulatory intervention path
- downside case tied to capital and confidence stress

Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.

## Default writing style

Write like a veteran bank analyst:
- lead with the actual driver of value or risk
- separate facts, interpretation, and variant view
- quantify balance-sheet and capital impacts whenever possible
- keep the tone skeptical, precise, and aware of accounting optics
- prefer “what matters, why it matters, what would change the view” over generic management-summary prose

When unsure, prioritize franchise durability, funding quality, and capital resilience over short-term EPS optics.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
