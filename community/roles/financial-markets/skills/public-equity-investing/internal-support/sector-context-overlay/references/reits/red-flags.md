# Red Flags And Failure Modes

Deferred reference for `REIT Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Red flags and common traps

Treat these as high-priority warning signals:
- AFFO defined too generously relative to real recurring capex or TI/LC
- same-store NOI growth driven by easy bad-debt compares, recoveries, or one-time fees
- leverage defended with optimistic asset sales or serial equity issuance
- near-term maturity wall with weak unsecured-market access
- capex-heavy sector valued on price/FFO without cash-leakage adjustment
- occupancy looks stable but leased-to-occupied gap, concessions, or bad debt is worsening
- tenant concentration or operator concentration hidden by master leases or aggregate reporting
- development pipeline sized for a better capital-market environment than exists today
- acquisition growth justified by "accretion" without a real spread over fully loaded cost of capital
- office or challenged-asset REIT using buybacks or dividend support to distract from rollover or obsolescence
- hotel or SHOP margin gains that are actually cycle-driven and capex-intensive
- straight-line rent or above-market lease amortization inflating perceived cash coverage
- management talking mostly about dividend yield while avoiding NAV, leverage, or recurring capex
- secured-debt creep eroding unsecured-bondholder protection
- asset marks or stated NAV relying on pre-rate-shock cap rates while debt still sits at higher refinancing costs
## Never do this

- Do not lead with P/E or generic EPS growth.
- Do not compare REITs across sectors on price/FFO without adjusting for capex, lease model, and balance sheet.
- Do not assume same-store NOI definitions are standardized.
- Do not accept AFFO as comparable across issuers without rebuilding it.
- Do not use occupancy alone as proof of pricing power or portfolio quality.
- Do not treat acquisition accretion as value creation unless the capital source and underwriting spread are clear.
- Do not discuss the dividend without discussing leverage, maturities, and retained cash.
- Do not analyze office or other challenged sectors without TI/LC and downtime.
- Do not analyze healthcare, hotel, or operating-style assets as if they were pure triple-net landlords.
- Do not analyze preferred or debt securities without mapping the claim position and unencumbered asset coverage.
- Do not confuse mortgage REITs with equity REITs.
