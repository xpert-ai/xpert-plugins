# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `Bank Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use sources in this order unless the task requires otherwise:
1. regulatory filings and statutory disclosures
2. annual and quarterly reports, earnings supplements, and investor-day materials
3. earnings transcript and management Q&A
4. regulator actions, consent orders, capital disclosures, Pillar 3, call reports, FR Y-9C, UBPR or local equivalents
5. rating-agency and sell-side framing
6. virtual data room materials: loan tapes, deposit studies, hedge books, ALCO materials, liquidity decks, top depositor lists, classified-loan migration, regulatory correspondence

## Reconciliation rules
- Reconcile company-adjusted metrics back to reported and regulatory numbers.
- Prefer average-balance disclosures to reverse-engineer NII.
- Prefer period-end disclosures for capital and liquidity snapshots.
- If management emphasizes one ratio, find the ratio it is trying to distract from.
## Bank-specific diligence requests

When the task involves diligence, request or prioritize the following if available:
- deposit tape by customer type, size bucket, insured status, geography, and rate paid
- top depositor list and concentration analysis
- loan tape with vintage, risk rating, geography, collateral type, maturity, coupon, and underwriting metrics
- criticized / classified migration by quarter
- office and CRE detail including refinance schedule and updated collateral values
- securities book by AFS/HTM, duration, unrealized marks, and pledgability
- ALM / rate-sensitivity deck and hedge inventory
- liquidity contingency plan and stress assumptions
- capital policy, buffer targets, and payout framework
- regulator correspondence, enforcement actions, audit findings, and remediation status
- legal-entity chart and debt maturity schedule
