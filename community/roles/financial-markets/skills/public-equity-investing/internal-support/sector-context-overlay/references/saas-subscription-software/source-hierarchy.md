# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `SaaS Subscription Software Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use sources in this order:

1. 10-K, 10-Q, annual report, earnings release, and official shareholder letter
2. earnings deck, KPI appendix, investor-day materials, and metric definitions
3. revenue-recognition footnotes, deferred-revenue and RPO disclosures, and SBC / cash-flow footnotes
4. management commentary on pricing, AI monetization, customer behavior, and GTM changes
5. pricing pages, product packaging disclosures, and channel / ecosystem details when available
6. peer filings for benchmarking and definition checks

## Reconciliation rules
- Reconcile revenue, ARR, billings, deferred revenue, and RPO before drawing conclusions.
- Never assume one company’s NRR, ARR, billings, or large-customer definition is comparable with another’s.
- When acquisitions are meaningful, separate organic growth from acquired growth everywhere.
- Normalize for FX, fiscal-calendar quirks, 53-week years, billing-cadence changes, and revenue-recognition shifts.
- If management stops disclosing a KPI, treat the absence as information rather than quietly substituting a weaker proxy.
- If a company has both subscription and consumption components, analyze them separately before recombining.
## SaaS-specific diligence requests

Request these when deeper underwriting is needed:

- ARR bridge by new, expansion, price, contraction, churn, acquisition, and FX
- Gross retention and NRR by customer size, cohort, geography, and product family
- Renewal calendar and upcoming concentration of large renewals
- Seat counts, seat growth, ARPU bridge, and seat-optimization history for seat-based models
- Committed usage, overage mix, rollover behavior, and usage concentration for consumption models
- Product attach and multi-product penetration by cohort
- Sales rep productivity, quota attainment, ramp curves, pipeline coverage, and channel contribution
- Contract-duration mix, annual-upfront versus monthly billing mix, and RPO aging
- Subscription gross-margin bridge including cloud, support, partner share, and AI inference costs
- Professional-services economics and whether services are required to land or retain customers
- Top-customer concentration and any exposure to one platform, vertical, or macro factor
- Capitalized commissions and renewal-commission policy
- AI monetization detail: pricing, attach, cost to serve, upsell behavior, and cannibalization risk
- Organic-versus-acquired growth bridge for roll-up stories
- Debt maturities, convert terms, and covenant definitions for credit work
