# Archetypes And Classification

Deferred reference for `Bank Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## First classify the bank

Classify the company before analyzing it. Do not compare banks mechanically across different business models.

## Archetypes

### Money-center / universal bank
- Core engines: broad deposit franchise, commercial banking, cards, markets, wealth, treasury services.
- Primary watch items: core NII versus market-sensitive revenues, capital return capacity, stress capital buffer, investment-banking and trading normalization, wholesale funding access.
- Common trap: mixing stable spread earnings with volatile capital-markets revenue and calling the result a clean core trend.

### Regional commercial / retail bank
- Core engines: spread income, commercial and consumer lending, branch-based deposits, treasury management, some wealth.
- Primary watch items: deposit mix and pricing, CRE concentration, securities marks, office exposure, core expense flexibility, M&A appetite.
- Common trap: treating deposit growth or NIM expansion as durable without testing beta, remix, and attrition.

### Community bank
- Core engines: local commercial lending, residential mortgage, small-business relationships, low-cost local deposits.
- Primary watch items: concentration risk, local economic exposure, succession/management quality, core deposit durability, expense burden.
- Common trap: over-trusting headline capital ratios without testing liquidity, concentration, and franchise depth.

### Custody / trust / transaction bank
- Core engines: servicing, custody, payments, treasury services, sweep balances, some spread income on operational deposits.
- Primary watch items: fee durability, operating deposit stickiness, rate sensitivity of noninterest-bearing balances, operational risk, regulatory/compliance spend.
- Common trap: applying standard regional-bank loan-credit frameworks to a fee-heavy franchise.

### Card / consumer lender with bank funding
- Core engines: card yields, revolving balances, interchange, late fees, retail deposits, unsecured consumer lending.
- Primary watch items: net charge-offs, delinquencies, payment rates, marketing intensity, customer acquisition quality, reserve sufficiency, funding costs.
- Common trap: misreading a late-cycle yield tailwind as true earnings quality while credit is quietly deteriorating.

### CRE-heavy / construction / specialty commercial lender
- Core engines: relationship lending, construction, multifamily, office, sponsor finance, niche vertical lending.
- Primary watch items: concentration limits, criticized/classified migration, refinancing walls, covenant quality, collateral marks, deposit concentration.
- Common trap: relying on benign current nonperformers while ignoring maturity walls and borrower equity erosion.

### Mortgage / servicing-heavy bank
- Core engines: mortgage originations, servicing income, spread banking, warehouse or correspondent channels.
- Primary watch items: gain-on-sale margins, servicing asset valuation, hedge effectiveness, prepayment assumptions, warehouse funding, rate sensitivity.
- Common trap: missing the difference between cyclical origination economics and more stable servicing income.

### Online / direct bank / brokered-deposit-heavy lender
- Core engines: high-yield deposits, online acquisition, concentrated lending niches, securities carry.
- Primary watch items: deposit price competition, uninsured and non-relationship funding, customer acquisition efficiency, liquidity contingency, mark-to-market risk.
- Common trap: mistaking fast growth for franchise quality when the liability side is rented rather than owned.

### Non-U.S. / IFRS bank
- Core engines: vary widely.
- Primary watch items: local capital and liquidity regime, structural hedge, IFRS 9 staging, sovereign exposure, MREL/TLAC, currency mismatches.
- Common trap: translating local metrics into U.S. language too loosely and missing accounting or regulatory differences.
