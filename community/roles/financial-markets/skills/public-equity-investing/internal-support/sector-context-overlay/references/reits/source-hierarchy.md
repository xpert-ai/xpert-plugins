# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `REIT Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use sources in this order unless the task requires otherwise:
1. annual reports, 10-Ks, 10-Qs, supplements, rent rolls, debt schedules, property-level statistics
2. earnings supplements, investor days, and supplemental KPI definitions
3. unsecured bond indentures, rating reports, and covenant packages
4. asset-level diligence materials, market studies, appraisals, JV agreements, and development budgets
5. tenant or operator credit materials, sales reports, coverage schedules, CMBS servicer data, and third-party market data

## Reconciliation rules
- Reconcile GAAP revenue and net income to cash NOI, FFO, and AFFO.
- Reconcile same-store definitions across periods and across peers; same-store pools are not automatically comparable.
- Reconcile leased versus occupied metrics.
- Reconcile acquisition cap rates and development yields to stabilized cash returns, not just stated day-one yields.
- Reconcile dividend safety to AFFO and to liquidity or refinancing needs.
- If management emphasizes NAV, verify the cap-rate assumptions against actual private-market evidence and sector fundamentals.
## REIT-specific diligence requests

When the task involves diligence, request or prioritize the following if available:
- full rent roll with tenant, industry, lease start and end, rent, square footage, escalators, options, security deposits, and concessions
- top-tenant schedule and operator coverage or EBITDAR coverage where relevant
- same-store bridge showing occupancy, pricing, bad debt, recoveries, and expense drivers
- TI/LC history and forward budget by asset and market
- recurring maintenance capex by asset type
- development and redevelopment pipeline with total cost, spend to date, yield on cost, preleasing, and stabilization timing
- debt maturity ladder, secured versus unsecured breakdown, covenant summary, and hedging schedule
- JV structure, promote or fee arrangements, and distribution waterfalls
- asset appraisal or broker-opinion packages, market studies, and supply-pipeline data
- tenant sales, occupancy cost, foot traffic, or operator KPIs where relevant
- hotel renovation schedules, property-improvement plans, brand agreements, and management contracts where relevant
- data-center power or capacity pipeline, commencements backlog, and customer concentration
- tower master lease and churn-exposure details
- healthcare operator financials, reimbursement mix, labor, and licensure issues
