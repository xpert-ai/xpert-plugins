# Archetypes And Classification

Deferred reference for `Consumer Internet Marketplaces Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## First classify the marketplace before analyzing it

Classify the company first. Do not compare all consumer-internet platforms with one template.

## Archetypes

### Horizontal third-party ecommerce marketplace
- Core engines: buyer frequency, seller breadth, listing quality, conversion, ads attach, trust, and take-rate discipline.
- Primary watch items: active buyers, active sellers, GMS per buyer, focus-category strength, promoted-listing penetration, and seller economics.
- Common trap: treating GMV growth as durable when it came mainly from AOV inflation, a temporary category spike, or a seller-fee reset.

### Vertical specialist marketplace
- Core engines: category authority, high-intent traffic, better curation, trusted supply, and premium monetization in a niche.
- Primary watch items: category liquidity, repeat rate, authentic supply, seller concentration, and whether niche leadership truly creates pricing power.
- Common trap: assigning horizontal-marketplace multiples to a niche category whose TAM or liquidity ceiling is lower.

### Managed local-services or labor marketplace
- Core engines: local density, service quality, worker or provider supply, conversion, scheduling efficiency, and trust.
- Primary watch items: geographic density, fill rate, cancellation rate, provider utilization, incentives, and customer rebooking.
- Common trap: valuing it like a pure software or classifieds model when the service layer introduces real operating complexity.

### Mobility, delivery, or local-commerce marketplace
- Core engines: monthly active consumers, order or trip frequency, cross-category use, membership penetration, dispatch efficiency, and logistics cost.
- Primary watch items: frequency, marketplace GOV, contribution profit, average delivery time, Dasher or courier supply, merchant density, and ad monetization.
- Common trap: reading GOV growth as value creation without checking whether incentives, insurance, and logistics intensity are rising just as fast.

### Travel marketplace or accommodations platform
- Core engines: room nights or nights booked, ADR, supply depth, global demand mix, cancellation behavior, and merchant-versus-agency mix.
- Primary watch items: room nights, gross bookings or GBV, take rate, cross-border mix, app penetration, and unearned-fee dynamics.
- Common trap: confusing ADR and FX tailwinds for true transaction growth, or confusing merchant-mix shift for real take-rate improvement.

### Classifieds or listings marketplace
- Core engines: audience scale, listing density, lead conversion, and monetization of seller or advertiser demand rather than direct transaction take rate.
- Primary watch items: listing growth, paying dealers or agents, ARPA, lead quality, renewal rates, and off-platform conversion.
- Common trap: applying GMV frameworks to a business that monetizes listings and leads rather than closed transactions.

### Recommerce or resale marketplace
- Core engines: inventory intake, authentication or trust, buyer engagement, seller acquisition, and category-specific liquidity.
- Primary watch items: seller acquisition cost, supply quality, return rates, authentication throughput, and contribution margins after reverse logistics.
- Common trap: calling the model asset-light when handling, verification, and returns introduce meaningful working-capital and operational friction.

### Hybrid marketplace plus ads, payments, or fintech platform
- Core engines: core commerce liquidity plus monetization through ads, payments, lending, or merchant services.
- Primary watch items: unique active buyers, payment penetration, ad load, checkout and fulfillment attach, and whether ancillary products deepen the moat.
- Common trap: giving the high multiple of a scaled fintech or ad platform to a company whose core marketplace still drives most of the economics.

### Hybrid first-party plus third-party platform
- Core engines: traffic and ecosystem advantages, marketplace mix shift, ads, and optionality from fulfillment and private-label adjacency.
- Primary watch items: gross versus net revenue mix, first-party inventory risk, seller-services profit pools, and segment contribution margins.
- Common trap: valuing the whole company like a pure marketplace when material first-party, logistics, or inventory exposure changes the model.

### Reacceleration, turnaround, or distressed marketplace
- Core engines: direct traffic durability, existing buyer reactivation, seller quality, cost reset, and stabilization of liquidity.
- Primary watch items: cohort retention, repeat buyers, paid-marketing dependency, negative operating leverage, and balance-sheet runway.
- Common trap: assuming one quarter of margin improvement proves the marketplace is healthy when liquidity may still be deteriorating.
