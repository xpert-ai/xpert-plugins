# Archetypes And Classification

Deferred reference for `Insurance Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## First classify the insurer

Classify the company before analyzing it. Do not compare insurers mechanically across different liability structures.

## Archetypes

### Personal-lines P&C writer
- Core engines: pricing, exposure growth, frequency and severity, claims handling, direct or agent distribution, investment income.
- Primary watch items: rate adequacy, repair and medical inflation, homeowner catastrophe exposure, retention, advertising efficiency, reinsurance.
- Common trap: treating premium growth and a better reported combined ratio as durable when current accident-year margin is still below adequate price.

### Commercial multiline P&C writer
- Core engines: commercial property, casualty, workers compensation, packages, surety, broker distribution.
- Primary watch items: rate versus loss-cost trend, casualty social inflation, reserve picks, broker retention, cat and large-loss exposure.
- Common trap: letting prior-year reserve releases hide weak current accident-year casualty economics.

### Specialty / E&S writer
- Core engines: niche underwriting, broker relationships, disciplined capacity, tailored terms, higher attachment points.
- Primary watch items: renewal pricing, submission quality, limit profile, large-loss frequency, reinsurance availability, hard-versus-soft market position.
- Common trap: capitalizing peak hard-market returns as if they are permanent.

### Global reinsurer / Bermuda cat writer
- Core engines: property catastrophe, casualty, specialty, retrocession, life and health reinsurance.
- Primary watch items: renewal pricing, attachment points, aggregate covers, trapped capital, PMLs, reserve shocks, collateral, retro counterparties.
- Common trap: annualizing a cat-light year or ignoring retrocession counterparty risk.

### Life and retirement / spread-based annuity writer
- Core engines: spread income on the general account, fee income on riders, portfolio management, product manufacturing, reinsurance.
- Primary watch items: new-money yields, cost of funds, credited-rate resets, surrender behavior, alternative asset mix, ALM, statutory capital, cash remittances.
- Common trap: mistaking temporary spread expansion or alternative-income strength for durable distributable earnings.

### Protection-oriented life / disability / group benefits writer
- Core engines: mortality and morbidity underwriting, persistency, employer relationships, group benefits, individual protection.
- Primary watch items: claim incidence and duration, pricing adequacy, disability recoveries, reinsurance, expense discipline.
- Common trap: ignoring assumption drift because GAAP earnings appear smooth.

### Multiline / composite insurer
- Core engines: mix of P&C, life, retirement, and sometimes asset-management-adjacent economics.
- Primary watch items: conglomerate discount, capital allocation, reserve complexity, inter-segment capital fungibility, disclosure quality.
- Common trap: netting a good spread business against weak casualty or vice versa and calling the group diversified.

### Monoline / title / mortgage / financial guaranty / runoff insurer
- Core engines: specialized underwriting, housing or credit-cycle exposure, runoff reserve releases, investment portfolio leverage.
- Primary watch items: claim emergence lag, concentration, litigation, capital, investment risk, regulatory constraints.
- Common trap: comparing to broad P&C peers on standard combined-ratio frameworks without adjusting for cycle and claim timing.

### Non-U.S. / IFRS 17 / Solvency II insurer
- Core engines: vary widely.
- Primary watch items: contractual service margin, insurance service result versus finance result, solvency ratio, contract boundary, volatility adjustment, local remittance rules.
- Common trap: translating IFRS 17 insurance revenue or CSM release directly into U.S. GAAP premium intuition.
