## Biotech and pharma cheat sheet

Use these as anchors, not as substitutes for reading the actual disclosure. Definitions, endpoint construction, and revenue treatment vary materially by issuer.

- IND: investigational new drug application that permits clinical testing to begin.
- NDA / BLA / sNDA / sBLA: the filing form matters because small molecules, biologics, and line extensions face different comparability, exclusivity, and competition frameworks.
- Phase 1 / 2 / 3: useful labels, but the real question is what has actually been learned, how controlled the evidence is, and whether the next study is registrational.
- Primary endpoint: the pre-specified outcome the study is designed to win on. This matters more than the best slide in a deck.
- Secondary endpoints: helpful only after confirming multiplicity handling and whether they are supportive or exploratory.
- ORR / CR / PR / DOR: response metrics often matter in oncology and some rare diseases, but they are not interchangeable with survival or long-term functional benefit.
- PFS / OS / event-free survival / relapse-free survival: time-to-event endpoints that demand close attention to censoring, follow-up duration, control-arm strength, and hazard-ratio interpretation.
- Surrogate endpoint: a marker used instead of direct clinical benefit. Valuable when validated; dangerous when weakly linked to real outcomes.
- p-value: tells you about statistical compatibility with the null under the specified test, not clinical importance, durability, or commercial value.
- Hazard ratio: directionally useful, but only meaningful with context on absolute benefit, medians, tails, and follow-up maturity.
- Biomarker-enriched population: may improve hit rate and label value, but also changes the commercial denominator and testing burden.
- PDUFA date: a review target date, not a guaranteed approval date.
- AdCom: an advisory committee meeting that can shape perception and labeling expectations even when the vote is nonbinding.
- CRL: complete response letter. A CRL is not one thing; it can signal clinical, statistical, manufacturing, facility-inspection, comparability, labeling, or post-marketing issues with very different repair times.
- Label: the actual economic asset. Approval without a commercially useful label can destroy the modeled value.
- REMS / black box / post-marketing requirement: constraints that can narrow adoption, raise cost, or preserve uncertainty.
- Gross-to-net: the difference between gross sales and recognized net product revenue after rebates, chargebacks, distribution fees, returns, and other deductions.
- WAC / list price / net price: never treat list price as realized economics.
- Peak sales: not a fact. It is an assumption that must be built from eligible patients, diagnosis rate, treatment rate, share, persistence, geography, and price net of gross-to-net.
- LOE: loss of exclusivity. This is often the most important date in mature pharma.
- Orange Book / Purple Book: tools for mapping patent and exclusivity timing, but not substitutes for reading the full IP and competition picture.
- Royalty interest / synthetic royalty / revenue interest financing: can look equity-like in good times and debt-like in bad times.
- Cash runway: the number of quarters until the company likely needs financing under realistic spend assumptions, not management's most optimistic presentation.

## Clinical, Launch, And Financing Metrics

## 1) Disease, mechanism, modality, and true unit of value
### Metrics to track
- prevalence, incidence, diagnosed population, and treated population
- biomarker-testing rate and positivity rate where relevant
- patient starts, new prescriptions, total prescriptions, doses, or infusions
- persistence, refill rates, discontinuations, and compliance where relevant
- share within indicated line of therapy
- partner sales and royalty-bearing sales where applicable

## 2) Evidence quality: trial design, endpoint discipline, and data interpretation
### Metrics to track
- enrollment size, screen-failure rate, dropout rate, and exposure duration
- primary and key secondary endpoint results
- p-values, confidence intervals, hazard ratios, and absolute benefit
- response rate, complete response rate, duration of response, progression-free survival, overall survival, or other relevant endpoint metrics
- discontinuations, dose modifications, serious adverse events, and treatment-related deaths
- durability metrics and follow-up time

## 3) Regulatory path, filing risk, and label economics
### Metrics to track
- data readout date, filing date, filing acceptance date, AdCom date, and PDUFA date
- expedited-program status and what it really changes
- likely label breadth and line-of-therapy scope
- post-marketing commitment or confirmatory-study burden
- facility inspection status or manufacturing readiness indicators where disclosed
- approval timing by geography

## 4) Commercial opportunity, patient flow, and market-access reality
### Metrics to track
- treated-patient growth and patient-start trends
- script trends, vials or units, refill rates, and persistence
- list price, net price, and gross-to-net percentage
- payer coverage, prior-authorization rate, and time to reimbursement where disclosed
- specialty-pharmacy or channel inventory days where relevant
- market share by line of therapy or segment

## 5) Manufacturing, supply chain, and CMC execution
### Metrics to track
- batch yield, capacity, throughput, and lot-failure indicators where available
- inventory on hand and inventory turns for commercial products
- COGS as a percentage of net sales
- site-activation count for advanced therapies where relevant
- time from order to treatment or release where relevant
- history of shortages, recalls, or inspection-related disclosures

## 6) IP, regulatory exclusivity, lifecycle management, and LOE
### Metrics to track
- key patent-expiry dates and exclusivity-expiry dates
- first expected generic or biosimilar entry date
- revenue exposed to LOE in the next 1, 3, and 5 years
- share of sales from new launches versus mature products
- royalty step-down dates and rates
- contribution from lifecycle extensions and follow-on assets

## 7) Financial quality: cash burn, revenue quality, and P&L interpretation
### Metrics to track
- ending cash, short-term investments, and net cash
- quarterly operating cash burn and normalized burn
- product revenue growth, royalty growth, and collaboration or milestone mix
- gross-to-net percentage and gross margin
- R&D, SG&A, and launch-investment trajectory
- cash runway in quarters under base and downside spend assumptions
- diluted shares outstanding and in-the-money converts or warrants

## 8) Capital structure, financing path, and public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff
### Metrics to track
- cash plus investments versus debt and royalty obligations
- net leverage for profitable issuers
- quarters of runway to next major catalyst and to required financing
- convert conversion prices, maturities, and potential dilution
- royalty burden as a share of product or partner revenue
- minimum liquidity and covenant headroom where relevant
- debt maturity ladder and interest coverage for mature issuers
