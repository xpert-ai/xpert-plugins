# KPI Cheat Sheet

Deferred reference for `Oil Gas E&P Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## E&P KPI cheat sheet

Use these definitions consistently and do not let management hide behind boe shorthand.

- Production: reported sales volumes or production volumes by commodity. Always separate oil, gas, and NGLs before recombining.
- BOE: barrel-of-oil-equivalent. Useful for scale, dangerous for value. A 6:1 gas conversion is not an economic equivalency.
- WI / NRI: working interest and net revenue interest. WI drives capex and operating exposure; NRI drives realized revenue.
- Realized price: what the company actually receives after benchmark move, location basis, quality, transport, marketing, and mix.
- Differential: the gap between benchmark price and realized wellhead or sales price.
- LOE: lease operating expense. Lifting cost. Must be separated from GPT and production taxes.
- GPT: gathering, processing, and transportation. Often the key hidden cost line, especially for gas and NGL names.
- DD&A: depletion, depreciation, and amortization. Useful, but not a substitute for reserve quality or full-cycle economics.
- PDP / PDNP / PUD: proved developed producing, proved developed non-producing, and proved undeveloped reserves.
- Reserve replacement ratio: net reserve additions divided by production. Distinguish total from organic.
- Production replacement: whether drilling and acquisitions are replacing decline in actual output, not just reserve bookings.
- Reserve life index: reserves divided by current production. Useful only after checking commodity mix and PUD quality.
- Standardized measure: GAAP discounted future net cash flow of proved reserves using SEC-style assumptions. Helpful for comparability, not fair value.
- PV-10: pre-tax present value of future net revenues from proved reserves discounted at 10%. Useful, but often excludes taxes and hedges and depends on the price deck.
- F&D / FD&A cost: finding and development, or finding, development, and acquisition cost per BOE added.
- Recycle ratio: margin per BOE divided by FD&A cost per BOE. Can be informative, but only if both numerator and denominator are clean.
- Maintenance capital: capital required to hold production approximately flat, after allowing for natural decline.

Interpretation rules:
- BOE growth is not valuable if the mix shifts toward lower-margin gas or NGL and differentials worsen.
- Production growth is not valuable if reserve quality, inventory duration, or free cash flow deteriorates.
- A high reserve replacement ratio is not automatically bullish if it came from acquisitions, price revisions, or low-quality PUDs.
- A low LOE number can be misleading if GPT, workover, water handling, or corporate costs sit elsewhere.
- PV-10 and standardized measure are tools, not answers. They are highly sensitive to price deck, development timing, reserve category, and tax treatment.
- Cheap EV / EBITDA or EV / cash flow can be a trap if the company has steep decline, shallow inventory, heavy transport commitments, or a looming maturity wall.

## E&P Operating Metric Map

## 1) Molecule, basin, and economic unit of account
### Metrics to track
- production by commodity and basin
- oil mix, gas mix, NGL mix
- WI and NRI by core asset where available
- realized prices by commodity
- benchmark-to-realized differential
- operated versus non-operated mix
- regional production concentration

## 2) Reserve quality, inventory depth, and development runway
### Metrics to track
- proved reserves by commodity
- reserve replacement ratio and organic reserve replacement ratio
- PDP, PDNP, and PUD mix
- reserve life index
- net reserve additions by source
- proved undeveloped conversion and aging
- location count by tier and by basin where disclosed
- type-curve assumptions, EURs, lateral lengths, and spacing

## 3) Production durability, decline, and maintenance capital
### Metrics to track
- production growth by commodity and basin
- new wells turned in line, completion cadence, and rig count where relevant
- base decline and corporate decline estimates
- maintenance capital estimate
- capex per incremental BOE or per incremental barrel of oil
- inventory years at current pace and maintenance pace
- DUC inventory and completion timing where relevant

## 4) Realized pricing, differentials, hedges, and transportation exposure
### Metrics to track
- realized price by commodity
- benchmark price by commodity
- cash hedge settlement and mark-to-market effect
- hedged volume as a percentage of expected production
- hedge tenor and strike structure
- GPT cost per BOE or per Mcfe
- firm transport commitments, reservation charges, and MVCs
- basis exposure and geographic price realization quality

## 5) Cost structure and capital efficiency
### Metrics to track
- LOE per BOE
- GPT per BOE or per Mcfe
- production taxes per BOE
- cash G&A per BOE
- D&C cost per well, per lateral foot, or per location
- corporate break-even and asset-level break-even
- FD&A or F&D cost per BOE
- recycle ratio
- DD&A per BOE

## 6) Balance sheet, liquidity, and capital allocation
### Metrics to track
- gross debt, net debt, and liquidity
- debt maturity schedule
- secured versus unsecured mix
- net debt / EBITDAX and interest coverage
- borrowing-base utilization where relevant
- shareholder returns versus capex versus debt paydown
- asset-retirement obligations and plugging liabilities
- fixed transport or midstream commitments

## 7) Earnings quality, reserve accounting, and normalization
### Metrics to track
- adjusted EBITDAX or cash flow
- hedge settlement and unrealized derivative impact
- reserve revisions from price versus other factors
- acquisition and divestiture contribution
- standardized measure and PV-10 disclosures where available
- per-share production, reserve, and free-cash-flow metrics
