# Valuation Rules

Deferred reference for `sector-context-overlay` saas-subscription-software sector lens. Load only when the task needs valuation, public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff, or Credit Markets handoff rules.

## Use recurring-software valuation, not generic tech shortcuts

## Equity valuation hierarchy

1. EV / next-twelve-month revenue for high-growth companies where revenue durability and scale advantages matter more than near-term margins.
2. EV / next-twelve-month gross profit when comparing businesses with very different gross-margin structures, especially consumption or AI-heavy models.
3. EV / free cash flow or FCF yield for mature companies with more stable growth and higher visibility.
4. Sum-of-the-parts for hybrid businesses with distinct subscription, usage, services, or legacy-license components.

### Valuation rules
- Higher multiples belong to businesses with strong gross retention, healthy NRR, durable multi-product expansion, efficient GTM, high incremental margins, and manageable dilution.
- Penalize companies whose growth is duration-driven, acquisition-driven, or overly dependent on one volatile usage vector.
- Normalize for constant currency, acquisitions, divestitures, and metric-definition changes before comparing peers.
- Revenue multiples are less useful when the company has structurally lower gross margin, heavy services mix, or AI economics that meaningfully depress contribution margin.
- FCF-based valuation is only credible when the cash flow is not being flattered by billing structure, restructuring, or aggressive SBC.

## Credit valuation hierarchy

1. Recurring-revenue durability and gross retention.
2. Free cash flow conversion after realistic cash costs.
3. Net leverage, liquidity runway, and debt maturity profile.
4. Covenant quality, issuer behavior, and acquisition appetite.
5. Recovery value based on software asset quality, customer concentration, and renewal durability.
