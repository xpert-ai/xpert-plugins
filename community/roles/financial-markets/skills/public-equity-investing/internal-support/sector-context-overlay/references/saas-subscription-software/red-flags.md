# Red Flags And Failure Modes

Deferred reference for `SaaS Subscription Software Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Red flags and common traps

- ARR grows while revenue and large-customer metrics do not, because the ARR definition changed.
- NRR stays healthy while gross retention weakens and new customer acquisition slows.
- Billings or cRPO outperforms mainly because contract durations lengthened.
- Deferred revenue weakens due to billing-cadence changes, but analysts misread it as demand deterioration.
- Management emphasizes AI ACV, AI ARR, seats, or tokens without showing durable paid usage and margin impact.
- Usage revenue spikes because of one customer, one incident, or one project rather than broad adoption.
- Professional services rise too quickly, suggesting product complexity or weak self-serve implementation.
- Free cash flow improves due to layoffs, tax timing, vendor stretch, or annual prepay mix rather than structural leverage.
- SBC remains extremely high while management celebrates non-GAAP margin.
- Large-deal strength masks weak broad-market demand and poorer logo retention.
- Partner-led growth accelerates, but the company gives up pricing control or margin.
- The company stops disclosing NRR, billings, or seat counts just as those metrics become harder to defend.
- A cloud-transition story gets multiple expansion credit even though the mix shift is largely migration of legacy maintenance revenue.
- Acquired growth is described as platform cross-sell before enough time has passed to prove it.
- Analysts value a consumption business on seat-based SaaS heuristics or vice versa.
## Never do this

- Never annualize one quarter of strong calculated billings into a durable demand thesis.
- Never quote NRR without also asking what gross retention and customer concentration look like.
- Never treat all backlog metrics as equally predictive across seat-based, monthly-billed, and consumption models.
- Never let non-GAAP margin or free cash flow stand in for unit economics without checking SBC and deferred-revenue support.
- Never compare software companies mechanically when one is seat-based, another is usage-based, and another is services-assisted.
- Never assume AI monetization is accretive before checking pricing, attach, and inference cost.
