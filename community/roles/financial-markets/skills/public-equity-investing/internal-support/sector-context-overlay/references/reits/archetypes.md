# Archetypes And Classification

Deferred reference for `REIT Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## First classify the REIT

Classify the company before analyzing it. Do not compare all REITs mechanically.

## Archetypes

### Net lease / triple-net REIT
- Core engines: contractual rent escalators, tenant credit, rent coverage, lease duration, capital recycling.
- Primary watch items: tenant concentration, lease-maturity walls, re-leasing residual value, CPI versus fixed escalators, sale-leaseback underwriting, acquisition spreads versus cost of capital.
- Common trap: calling the cash flow bond-like without underwriting unit-level tenant health, rent coverage, and real-estate residual value.

### Shopping center / open-air retail REIT
- Core engines: grocer or necessity-anchor health, small-shop leasing, occupancy, tenant sales, mark-to-market rents, recoveries.
- Primary watch items: anchor renewal risk, co-tenancy, bad debt, leasing spreads, redevelopment yield.
- Common trap: focusing on headline occupancy without tenant sales productivity, occupancy cost, or weak inline leasing economics.

### Mall / outlet REIT
- Core engines: tenant sales per square foot, occupancy, releasing spreads, mixed-use redevelopment, foot traffic.
- Primary watch items: department-store box exposure, occupancy cost, redevelopment capex, tenant bankruptcies.
- Common trap: treating high sales per square foot or stable occupancy as enough without measuring how much capex and redevelopment spend are required to sustain it.

### Industrial / logistics REIT
- Core engines: occupancy, cash rent change, net effective rent change, retention, development, market-rent mark-to-market.
- Primary watch items: supply deliveries, tenant demand, lease duration, land bank, development yields, customer concentration.
- Common trap: annualizing peak rent spreads after supply turns or ignoring development risk.

### Apartment / residential REIT
- Core engines: same-store revenue growth from occupancy, rate, concessions, and bad debt; expense control; development delivery.
- Primary watch items: supply wave, affordability, resident turnover, concessions, bad debt, local regulation, taxes and insurance, blended lease rates.
- Common trap: trusting average rent growth without separating occupancy, concessions, bad debt, and expense inflation.

### Self-storage REIT
- Core engines: street rates versus in-place rates, occupancy, move-in and move-out cadence, rate optimization, brand and digital demand capture.
- Primary watch items: occupancy trajectory, realized rent per occupied square foot, marketing spend, new supply, acquisition integration.
- Common trap: missing that near-term revenue can rise or fall because management is trading off occupancy versus price.

### Healthcare REIT
- Core engines: varies by subsector.
- Triple-net MOB, SNF, hospital, lab style: rent coverage, tenant or operator health, lease duration, reimbursement sensitivity.
- SHOP or RIDEA seniors housing style: occupancy, RevPOR, labor cost, margin, local supply, acuity.
- Primary watch items: operator concentration, reimbursement, labor, capex, asset obsolescence, government-policy sensitivity.
- Common trap: analyzing all healthcare REITs like net lease when a large share of value may sit in operating assets.

### Lodging / hotel REIT
- Core engines: RevPAR, ADR, occupancy, food and beverage or ancillary revenue, hotel EBITDA margin, asset management, capex.
- Primary watch items: group versus transient mix, market mix, renovation schedule, brand fees, labor, insurance, cyclicality.
- Common trap: using landlord-style NOI logic on what is effectively a daily mark-to-market operating business with heavy recurring capex.

### Data center / digital infrastructure REIT
- Core engines: signed bookings, commencements, backlog, power availability, interconnection, rent escalators, development pipeline.
- Primary watch items: power procurement, customer concentration, lease-commencement lag, capital intensity, competition, technology obsolescence.
- Common trap: treating backlog as current earnings without modeling the lag, capital spend, and customer concentration.

### Tower / communications infrastructure REIT
- Core engines: organic tenant billings growth, colocations, amendment activity, churn, escalators, international FX, ground-lease economics.
- Primary watch items: carrier spending cycles, churn from consolidations, contractual escalators, emerging-technology impact, zoning moat.
- Common trap: comparing tower REITs directly to traditional landlord REITs on simple occupancy or cap-rate logic.

### Office / lab / challenged-asset REIT
- Core engines: lease rollover, tenant credit, amenity and capex intensity, market vacancy, redevelopment or conversion potential.
- Primary watch items: near-term rollover, TI/LC burden, downtime, obsolescence, lender posture, tax reassessment.
- Common trap: accepting a low multiple or high dividend as enough without testing liquidation value and refinancing risk.

### Specialty / gaming / experiential REIT
- Core engines: tenant or operator coverage, master leases, escalators, regional demand, transaction underwriting.
- Primary watch items: operator concentration, asset fungibility, regulatory risk, financing receivables, rent coverage, acquisition yields.
- Common trap: treating long lease duration as safety when asset use is narrow or tenant or operator concentration is high.

### Mortgage REIT
Not covered by this skill. Mortgage REITs are financing spread vehicles, not property-cash-flow landlords. Treat mREIT support as future or non-local unless a dedicated real estate credit or mREIT workflow is explicitly available.
