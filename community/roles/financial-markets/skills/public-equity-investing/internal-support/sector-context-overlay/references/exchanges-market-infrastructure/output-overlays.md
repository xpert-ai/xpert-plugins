# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `Exchanges Market Infrastructure Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
Focus on what is normalized versus temporary, what deserves premium multiple support, and where consensus is misreading the mix between recurring and cyclical earnings.

## Event-driven
Prioritize earnings surprises driven by volume, capture, or rate-sensitive balances; M&A and integration; fee changes; regulatory decisions; lawsuits; product launches; and outage or cyber events.

## Macro
Treat the name as an expression of volatility, rate path, issuance cycle, passive flows, energy or commodity activity, and funding conditions. The key is identifying which macro variable matters most for that specific franchise.

## Quant / systematic
Normalize for regime effects. Raw volume, earnings momentum, and margin expansion can be misleading unless adjusted for volatility, turnover, rates, and one-time event periods. Use product-mix and regime controls where possible.

## Credit Markets handoff / special-situation credit context
Focus on holdco leverage, structural subordination, acquisition debt, contingent legal and cyber exposures, technology underinvestment, and any path where regulatory intervention or operational failure impairs cash flow.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
Spend more time on secular electronification, workflow embedding, data monetization, cross-sell, index and analytics expansion, and the quality of new product launches. Do not let a growth narrative override careful normalization of event-driven revenues.
## Output overlays for common tasks

## For a post-earnings deep dive
Lead with:
1. what changed in volume, mix, capture, and recurring growth
2. what portion of the quarter was structural versus volatility-driven
3. what changed in clearing, collateral, and rate-sensitive economics
4. whether competitive position improved or simply benefited from the tape
5. whether guidance implies normalization or continued favorable conditions

## For a pre-earnings preview
Focus on:
1. current monthly volume trends and product mix
2. expected volatility and funding backdrop
3. listings and issuance environment where relevant
4. likely direction of capture, FPM, or rebates
5. any regulatory, outage, or product-launch catalyst around the print

## For an investment memo
State explicitly:
1. the company archetype
2. the exact revenue mix between recurring, transactional, and rate-sensitive economics
3. the moat source
4. the normalized earnings base
5. the key debate, what the market is misunderstanding, and what would falsify the thesis

## For a financial model
The model must show:
1. segment-level revenue drivers
2. product-level or channel-level activity assumptions
3. monetization assumptions by line
4. explicit rate and volatility sensitivities
5. capital allocation and security-level implications

## When debt or structured work is requested, route to Credit Markets
Prioritize:
1. entity structure and upstreaming
2. downside free cash flow after required investment
3. event risk from operational and regulatory failure
4. concentration and earnings cyclicality
5. whether regulated or ring-fenced subsidiaries limit creditor access to value

Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.

## Style rules for using this sector pack

- Always say what exact business you are analyzing before offering an opinion.
- Prefer net economics over gross activity.
- Normalize by regime; do not let one exceptional quarter define the franchise.
- Use the company’s own metric definitions, then translate them into a comparable framework only after the definitions are confirmed.
- Call out what is benchmark, what is fungible, and what is recurring.
- Separate true moat from temporary stress-driven demand.
- For security-level underwriting, map the entity and the regulation before talking about downside protection.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
