# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `Oil Gas E&P Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use sources in this order unless the task requires otherwise:
1. annual reports, 10-Ks, 10-Qs, reserve disclosures, reserve engineer reports, hedge tables, debt schedules, and basin maps
2. earnings presentations, conference-call commentary, investor days, and supplemental operating statistics
3. credit agreements, bond documents, rating materials, and midstream or transport disclosures
4. VDR engineering reports, A&D marketing materials, well-level data, lease maps, and internal reserve cases
5. basin data, state filings, commodity and basis data, service-cost benchmarks, and third-party engineering or operating comparisons

## Reconciliation rules
- Reconcile production volumes to sales volumes when they differ materially.
- Reconcile benchmark prices to realized prices commodity by commodity.
- Reconcile reserve additions into organic, acquired, price-driven, and technical buckets.
- Reconcile capital spending to wells turned in line, locations developed, and maintenance-capital needs.
- Reconcile corporate BOE metrics back to molecule-specific economics.
- Reconcile NAV or PV discussions to the specific price deck and tax treatment used.
- Reconcile hedged volumes and settlement values to forecast production and liquidity needs.
## E&P-specific diligence requests

When the task involves diligence, request or prioritize the following if available:
- full reserve report with engineer assumptions, by basin, commodity, and reserve category
- reserve roll-forward and detail on revisions due to price versus other factors
- inventory deck by location tier, lateral length, spacing, and development timing
- type curves, EUR assumptions, spacing studies, parent-child and cube-development analysis
- well-level production history, turn-in-line dates, and operating downtime history
- capex budget by drill, complete, facilities, land, and infrastructure category
- operated versus non-operated asset split and partner schedule control
- acreage maps, HBP status, lease expirations, royalty burdens, and title issues
- gathering, processing, transport, and marketing contracts, including MVCs and reservation charges
- hedge book by commodity, tenor, and instrument type
- debt documents, borrowing-base calculations, collateral package, and covenant summary
- abandonment, plugging, water handling, environmental, and reclamation obligations
- acquisition or divestiture models and synergy cases if M&A is part of the story
- for international assets: PSC, concession, tax, and repatriation terms
