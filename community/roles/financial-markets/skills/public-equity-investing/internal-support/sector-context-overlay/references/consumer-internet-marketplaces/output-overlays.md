# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `Consumer Internet Marketplaces Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
- Focus on cohort durability, liquidity quality, take-rate sustainability, ad and ancillary monetization quality, margin durability, and whether the moat is getting stronger or weaker.

## Event-driven
- Focus on beat-and-raise quality, fee or pricing changes, regulatory decisions, M and A, strategic pivots into new categories, activist angles, convert overhangs, and whether margin moves came from structural improvements or transient incentive changes.

## Macro
- Focus on consumer-discretionary sensitivity, travel trends, labor availability, fuel and insurance costs where relevant, FX, cross-border demand, ad spend sensitivity, and whether the platform is skewed to discretionary or nondiscretionary categories.

## Quant or systematic
- Standardize around active-demand growth, order or booking growth, GMV growth, take-rate trend, contribution-margin trend, marketing efficiency, free-cash-flow conversion, dilution, and estimate-revision direction. Be careful with definitional changes.

## Credit Markets handoff / structured-finance context
- Focus on downside cash generation, fixed-cost flexibility, working-capital reversal risk, regulated-entity constraints, legal exposures, debt maturities, convert overhang, and whether the platform could self-fund through a downturn.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
- Focus on category expansion, geographic density, new-supply onboarding, cross-category behavior, ad and fintech attach, and whether the platform can expand the lifetime value of both sides without destroying economics.
## Output overlays for common tasks

## For a post-earnings deep dive
- Lead with the GMV bridge: active demand, frequency, ticket size, and take rate.
- State clearly whether the quarter was driven by better liquidity, better cohorts, higher monetization, or accounting and mix noise.
- Separate the core marketplace from ads, subscriptions, payments, logistics, or fintech.
- Call out any mix shifts, incentive changes, or metric-definition changes that distort comparability.
- End with the debate: why bulls think the network is getting stronger and why bears think monetization is outrunning liquidity.

## For a pre-earnings preview
- Focus on the setup around demand growth, supply density, take-rate cadence, promotions, category mix, travel or seasonal timing, and contribution-margin expectations.
- Identify which metrics the market is most likely to key on for that name.
- State whether the quarter is more likely to hinge on liquidity, ad monetization, fulfillment efficiency, or working-capital optics.

## For an investment memo
- Make the core debate about network strength, not just about revenue growth.
- State the scarce side of the marketplace and why that matters.
- Include a clean bull, base, and bear case around cohorts, liquidity, take-rate sustainability, and contribution-margin progression.
- Be explicit about what portion of value belongs to the core marketplace versus ancillary layers.

## For a financial model
- Build a bottom-up GMV or gross-bookings model tied to the actual transaction architecture.
- Model take-rate components separately.
- Explicitly capture incentives, logistics, trust and safety, and working-capital dynamics.
- Include scenario cases for cohort strength, supply density, take-rate changes, and contribution margin.

## When debt or structured work is requested, route to Credit Markets
- Prioritize downside liquidity, working-capital reversal risk, regulatory liabilities, fixed-cost flexibility, and normalised cash generation.
- Treat customer funds and restricted balances carefully.
- Evaluate whether the company can continue investing enough to protect liquidity without relying on external capital.
Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.
## Style rules for using this sector pack

- Write like a veteran internet and marketplace analyst, not like a generic digital-business summarizer.
- Define the company's own KPIs before using them in an argument.
- Separate facts, management framing, and your inference.
- Be explicit about which side of the network is scarce and which side is monetized.
- Distinguish demand quality, supply quality, and monetization quality instead of rolling them into one word like engagement.
- When the company is hybrid, present the marketplace, ads, payments, logistics, and first-party pieces separately before combining them.
- Treat the business as a network and unit-economics problem, not just a revenue-growth story.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
