# Valuation Rules

Deferred reference for `sector-context-overlay` exchanges-market-infrastructure sector lens. Load only when the task needs valuation, public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff, or Credit Markets handoff rules.

## Use exchange and market infrastructure-specific valuation, not generic financial-company shortcuts

## Equity valuation hierarchy

### For listed derivatives and clearing-heavy franchises
- Use EV to EBITDA, P/E, and free-cash-flow yield on normalized rather than peak activity.
- Cross-check with a scenario-weighted view of volatility, rates, and collateral economics.
- Give credit for benchmark products and clearing moats only if concentration risk and fee durability are understood.

### For diversified exchange and recurring-data groups
- Use sum-of-the-parts where warranted: transaction businesses, data and connectivity, indices, listings, and workflow or software.
- Higher-multiple recurring segments deserve separate treatment only if retention, pricing power, and margin durability are real.
- Do not award software multiples to recurring lines that still depend heavily on markets activity or capital-markets issuance.

### For electronic trading venues
- Use EV to EBITDA, P/E, and revenue-multiple cross-checks, but anchor them to normalized FPM and share rather than just volume growth.
- Distinguish between structurally stronger network effects and cyclical revenue from high-volatility periods.

### Valuation rules
- Never capitalize peak crisis or event-driven volume without a reversion case.
- Separate recurring revenue from transactional and rate-sensitive revenue explicitly.
- Normalize collateral-related earnings and interest income instead of blindly annualizing them.
- Apply premium multiples only to businesses with durable pricing power, benchmark products, or high-retention recurring revenue.
- Penalize businesses where share is bought through rebates or where economics are concentrated in one product set that may be vulnerable.

## Credit valuation hierarchy

- Start with entity-level cash flow, not consolidated optics.
- Prioritize free cash flow after required resiliency, compliance, and technology investment.
- Underwrite operational and regulatory event risk explicitly.
- Treat systemic-importance status as both a support and a complication: it can stabilize the franchise while also increasing supervisory burden and consequence of failure.
- For subordinated or structurally junior securities, be conservative about upstreaming and ring-fenced subsidiary economics.
