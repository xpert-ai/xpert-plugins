# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `Insurance Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use sources in this order unless the task requires otherwise:
1. statutory statements, annual statements, solvency or RBC disclosures, actuarial opinions, regulator filings
2. annual and quarterly reports, earnings supplements, and investor-day materials
3. reserve triangles, cat or PML disclosures, reinsurance-program summaries, earnings transcripts
4. rating-agency commentary and local solvency disclosures
5. virtual data room materials: loss triangles, bordereaux, treaty summaries, asset tapes, ALM or hedge decks, ORSA-style stress materials, actuarial memos

## Reconciliation rules
- Reconcile gross written, net written, net earned, and insurance revenue. They are not interchangeable.
- Reconcile GAAP or IFRS earnings to statutory capital and holdco cash generation.
- Reconcile adjusted operating metrics back to reserve movements, catastrophe load, and investment marks.
- For life insurers, separate premiums from deposits and account-value flows.
- If management emphasizes book-value growth, check whether the asset book, reserve position, and rating capital actually support it.
## Insurance-specific diligence requests

When the task involves diligence, request or prioritize the following if available:
- reserve triangles by line, accident year, paid, incurred, case, and large-loss detail
- actuarial reserve reviews, carried-versus-indicated reserve analysis, and adverse-development commentary
- rate change, retention, submission, and exposure data by line and geography
- catastrophe exposure by peril, geography, attachment point, and modeled PML or tail return periods
- reinsurance treaties, attachments, covers, counterparty concentrations, and collateral terms
- investment holdings tape by asset class, rating, duration, liquidity, CRE, private credit, structured, and affiliated exposure
- ALM, liquidity, and hedge deck including collateral stress
- statutory entity chart, dividend restrictions, RBC or solvency by entity, and holdco debt maturity schedule
- policyholder behavior studies: lapse, surrender, mortality, morbidity, disability incidence, and duration
- rating-agency correspondence and capital framework
- affiliated reinsurance, funds withheld, modco, reserve financing, and side-letter summary if relevant
