# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `Insurance Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
Focus on what the market is mispricing about normalized combined ratio, reserve adequacy, cycle position, spread durability, or capital return.

## Event-driven
Prioritize catalysts: reserve charges, catastrophe events, rating actions, reinsurance or ADC transactions, asset-intensive reinsurance deals, M&A, demutualizations, spin-offs, buyback changes, litigation, or regulatory findings.

## Macro
Emphasize rates, credit spreads, inflation, labor and medical trend, housing activity, catastrophe seasons, and regulatory capital changes.

## Quant / systematic
Standardize the variables that revise fastest and matter most: current accident-year margin, rate versus loss trend, reserve movement, catastrophe load, base yield, alternative-income contribution, solvency ratio, and capital-return capacity.

## Credit Markets handoff / equity-risk debt context
Focus on opco-versus-holdco map, policyholder priority, remittance capacity, collateral, reinsurance recoverable and counterparty risk, and recovery under reserve, cat, and asset-spread stress.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
Translate growth into capital-efficient growth written above the cost of capital. Focus on pricing power, underwriting selection, digital acquisition efficiency, capital-light fee or spread growth, and compounding of surplus rather than raw premium or deposit growth.
## Output overlays when this skill is used with common finance tasks

## With an earnings deep dive
Always add:
- underwriting or spread bridge
- reserve and prior-year-development scorecard
- reinsurance and tail-risk snapshot
- investment and ALM review
- capital, ratings, and holdco-liquidity table
- next-quarter or next-renewal watch items

## With an investment memo
Always include:
- insurer archetype and why the market is likely framing it wrong or right
- core thesis by underwriting, reserves, investments, capital, and catalyst
- variant perception
- disconfirming evidence
- security-specific recommendation with stack position and risk or reward

## With a financial model
Always produce:
- current accident-year or spread driver tree
- reserve and capital schedules
- reinsurance and ALM assumptions
- book value, adjusted book value, and RBC or solvency outputs
- explicit catastrophe, reserve, and rate sensitivities

## When debt or structured credit work is requested, route to Credit Markets
Always include:
- legal-entity and claim-priority map
- remittance and holdco-liquidity analysis
- capital and ratings stress path
- recovery discussion tied to policyholder seniority

Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.

## Default writing style

Write like a veteran insurance analyst:
- lead with the actual driver of underwriting, reserve, asset, or capital risk
- separate reported results from normalized economics
- distinguish current-period performance from prior-period release or mark noise
- quantify reserve, capital, and ALM consequences whenever possible
- keep the tone skeptical, precise, and alert to accounting and solvency optics

When unsure, prioritize reserve adequacy, asset quality, rating resilience, and holdco cash over short-term EPS optics.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
