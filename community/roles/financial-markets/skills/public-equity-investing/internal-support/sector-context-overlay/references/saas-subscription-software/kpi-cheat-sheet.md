# KPI Cheat Sheet

Deferred reference for `SaaS Subscription Software Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## SaaS KPI cheat sheet

Use these as interpretation anchors, not as universal truths. Definitions vary materially by issuer.

- ARR: annualized recurring revenue run-rate of the installed base. Always test what is included, when it is measured, whether usage overages are embedded, and whether services, term licenses, or pilot contracts are excluded.
- ACV: annualized contract value. Useful for large-deal tracking and sales productivity, but only after confirming whether it is gross or net of services, one-time fees, and channel economics.
- MRR: monthly recurring revenue. More useful in SMB and PLG businesses than in large-enterprise multi-year contract models.
- Dollar-based net retention / NRR / DBNRR: existing-customer revenue retention including expansion, price, contraction, and churn. Never read it without gross retention.
- Gross retention / gross revenue retention: installed-base durability before upsell. Often more informative than NRR when optimization or churn risk is rising.
- Logo retention: customer-count retention. Most useful in SMB and PLG; less useful than revenue retention in enterprise models.
- Billings / calculated billings: revenue plus change in deferred revenue. Helpful but noisy because invoice timing, contract duration, annual prepay mix, acquisitions, and FX can distort a single quarter.
- Deferred revenue: invoiced but not yet recognized revenue. Best interpreted in annual-upfront enterprise models; less informative in monthly or consumption-heavy models.
- RPO / cRPO: total and current remaining performance obligations. Better backlog indicators than deferred revenue for enterprise models, but quality depends on contract duration, cancellation terms, and usage mechanics.
- Rule of 40 / Rule of 50: shorthand for growth plus margin. Only useful after checking whether the margin is real and how much dilution is being used to generate it.
- Magic Number / CAC payback: useful for GTM efficiency, but only after adjusting for acquisitions, capitalization of commissions, channel-heavy motions, and unusual seasonality.
- Large-customer counts: customers above a disclosed ARR or ACV threshold. Valuable for monitoring enterprise penetration if the company keeps definitions consistent.

## Recurring Software Metric Map

## 1) Monetization architecture and the true unit of account
### Metrics to track
- paid seats or paid users
- ARPU or ARPA
- committed ARR or committed usage
- overage revenue as a percentage of total revenue
- multi-product customer count or attach rate
- AI attach rate where disclosed

## 2) Installed-base quality: ARR, customers, and retention durability
### Metrics to track
- ARR growth and organic ARR growth
- customer count by disclosed thresholds
- customers above $100k, $500k, or $1m ARR or ACV where disclosed
- gross retention and logo retention
- NRR / DBNRR
- customer concentration

## 3) Expansion behavior and cohort quality
### Metrics to track
- NRR by customer bucket if disclosed
- cohort ARR or cohort revenue curves
- products per customer or attach rate
- seat growth versus price / ARPU growth
- usage growth versus committed-capacity growth
- expansion contribution to total ARR growth

## 4) Bookings, billings, RPO, duration, and revenue recognition
### Metrics to track
- billings or calculated billings
- current and noncurrent deferred revenue
- total RPO and cRPO
- implied duration from RPO mix where possible
- annual upfront billing mix
- revenue-conversion rate from beginning backlog

## 5) Go-to-market quality and sales efficiency
### Metrics to track
- S&M as a percentage of revenue
- Magic Number or CAC payback where relevant
- net new ARR per sales rep or per quota carrier if available
- enterprise pipeline coverage and win rates
- partner-sourced or channel-sourced mix
- average contract value and large-deal count

## 6) Gross-margin architecture and the true cost to serve
### Metrics to track
- subscription gross margin
- professional-services gross margin
- total gross margin
- infrastructure and hosting cost as a percentage of revenue
- AI serving cost or inference cost where disclosed
- services mix and support intensity

## 7) Operating leverage, cash conversion, and dilution quality
### Metrics to track
- GAAP and non-GAAP operating margin
- operating cash flow margin and free cash flow margin
- SBC as a percentage of revenue
- diluted share-count growth
- contract-acquisition-cost asset growth and amortization
- capex as a percentage of revenue

## 8) Product moat, competition, and AI transition risk
### Metrics to track
- module attach or suite penetration
- % of customers on higher-value editions
- developer / partner ecosystem indicators
- product-adoption or usage intensity metrics
- AI-specific adoption metrics only when tied to paid usage or paid seats

## 9) Guidance quality, linearity, and revision risk
### Metrics to track
- implied next-quarter and full-year revenue growth
- implied margin progression
- cRPO growth versus revenue growth
- large-deal count or large-customer growth trends
- guidance changes to billings, ARR, or retention where disclosed

## 10) Capital structure and public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff
### Metrics to track
- gross and net leverage
- cash runway and liquidity
- recurring-revenue leverage where relevant
- interest coverage or cash interest burden
- convert maturity schedule and potential dilution
- acquisition capacity
