# KPI Cheat Sheet

Deferred reference for `Consumer Internet Marketplaces Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Consumer internet and marketplace KPI cheat sheet

Use these as interpretation anchors, not as universal truths. Definitions vary materially by issuer.

- GMV / GMS / GOV / gross bookings / GBV: total transaction value flowing through the platform. Always normalize for taxes, shipping, tips, refunds, cancellations, and off-platform activity before comparing companies.
- Take rate / revenue take rate / net revenue margin: revenue as a percentage of GMV or GOV. Always break into transaction fees, ads, subscriptions, payments, fulfillment, financing, or other services.
- Active buyers / active users / active consumers / active bookers / MAPCs: demand-side counts. Never compare a monthly metric to a trailing-twelve-month metric without adjustment.
- Active sellers / merchants / hosts / couriers / listings supply: supply-side counts. Volume counts are not enough; test liquidity and quality of supply.
- Orders / trips / room nights / items sold / bookings: the transaction unit that maps the network's activity into volume growth.
- Order frequency / purchase frequency / booking frequency / repeat rate: measures repeat demand quality.
- AOV / ADR / basket size / average gross booking per transaction: the ticket-size component of GMV growth.
- Cohort retention / reactivation / resurrection: the durability of demand after initial acquisition.
- Supply retention / merchant retention / host retention: the durability of the seller or partner base.
- Contribution profit / variable contribution: a better lens than GAAP gross margin for managed marketplaces with logistics or support intensity.
- CAC / payback / LTV: useful only after separating paid acquisition from direct traffic, brand, SEO, and referrals.
- Ads penetration / promoted listings / sponsored placement mix: monetization layers that can be attractive but can also mask weaker core demand.
- Payments, fulfillment, insurance, or financing attach: ancillary layers that can improve monetization but change risk and capital intensity.
- Refunds / chargebacks / fraud / cancellations / disputes: quality-control metrics that often worsen before headline KPIs show strain.
- Working-capital float: customer funds, unearned revenue, seller payables, and timing balances that can flatter cash flow.

## Marketplace Liquidity And Unit-Economics Metrics

## 1) Transaction architecture and the true unit of account
### Metrics to track
- GMV / GMS / GOV / gross bookings / GBV
- orders, trips, room nights, or items sold
- active buyers or active consumers
- active sellers, merchants, hosts, or listings
- AOV, ADR, or average booking per order
- take rate by revenue component

## 2) Liquidity, density, and matching quality
### Metrics to track
- fill rate or successful-match rate
- sell-through or booking conversion
- average delivery or response time
- active listings per active buyer where relevant
- orders or transactions per active seller or merchant
- cancellation, refund, or defect rates

## 3) Demand acquisition, cohorts, and repeat behavior
### Metrics to track
- active buyers, MAUs, MAPCs, or active bookers
- new, repeat, and reactivated user counts
- order frequency or booking frequency
- GMS or GMV per active buyer
- retention by cohort and mature-cohort curves
- paid versus direct traffic mix where available

## 4) Supply quality, incentives, and partner economics
### Metrics to track
- active sellers, merchants, hosts, or providers
- listings, selection, or available inventory
- seller or merchant retention and churn
- take rate paid by the supply side
- supply acquisition cost
- average earnings or utilization where relevant

## 5) GMV growth decomposition and quality
### Metrics to track
- GMV or gross-booking growth
- active-buyer or active-consumer growth
- order, trip, or room-night growth
- average order value or ADR
- category and geographic growth mix
- organic versus acquired growth

## 6) Take rate, ad load, and monetization layers
### Metrics to track
- revenue take rate or net revenue margin
- core transaction take rate
- ads as a percentage of revenue or GMV
- payments or checkout penetration
- membership or subscription penetration
- merchant-versus-agency or gross-versus-net mix where relevant

## 7) Contribution economics, fulfillment, and trust and safety
### Metrics to track
- contribution profit and contribution margin where disclosed
- variable cost per order, trip, or booking
- payment-processing costs
- promotions and incentives as a percentage of GOV or revenue
- support, refund, or trust-and-safety costs
- on-time, defect, and customer-satisfaction metrics where disclosed

## 8) Competitive position, network effects, and platform power
### Metrics to track
- direct versus paid traffic mix
- cross-category attach or multi-product usage
- app penetration and repeat usage
- seller or merchant concentration
- active supply overlap where disclosed
- customer acquisition cost by channel

## 9) Cash flow quality, working capital, and balance-sheet reality
### Metrics to track
- operating cash flow and free cash flow
- restricted cash and customer funds
- seller, merchant, or host payable balances
- unearned revenue or deferred fees where relevant
- capex and lease obligations
- SBC and diluted-share growth

## 10) Capital structure and public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff
### Metrics to track
- gross and net leverage
- liquidity and restricted cash
- debt and convert maturity schedule
- fixed-cost base and cash-burn sensitivity
- normalized free cash flow under slower growth
- potential dilution from SBC and converts
