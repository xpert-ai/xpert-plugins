# Exchange Red Flags And Failure Modes

Deferred reference for `Exchanges Market Infrastructure Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Activity and Fee Traps

- Treating all ADV/ADNV as equal or annualizing peak volatility and crisis turnover.
- Ignoring rebates, liquidity payments, maker-taker economics, royalty fees, regulatory pass-throughs, or direct clearing costs.
- Assuming RPC, FPM, or net capture holds when mix shifts toward low-yield contracts, compression, portfolio trading, or automated flow.
- Missing fee-schedule changes, volume tiers, liquidity incentives, contract-value changes, or proprietary-versus-fungible product mix.
- Confusing gross share gain with profitable share gain.

## Clearing and Default Waterfall Traps

- Valuing a clearing-heavy franchise without mapping margin methodology, initial margin, variation margin, default fund, CCP skin-in-the-game, assessment powers, recovery tools, and loss-allocation sequence.
- Ignoring clearing-member concentration, client-clearing exposure, wrong-way risk, stressed liquidity needs, settlement-bank dependence, or operational capacity during default management.
- Treating guaranty-fund resources as parent economics or assuming member mutualization protects shareholders without reputational, litigation, or regulatory support risk.
- Missing collateral eligibility changes, procyclical margin calls, cross-margining changes, or recovery-and-resolution rules that alter customer behavior.

## Collateral, Rate, and Balance Traps

- Treating collateral spread, treasury income, or margin-cash interest as recurring fee revenue without rate-cycle and pass-through sensitivity.
- Ignoring whether collateral economics accrue to the parent, a regulated subsidiary, members, or customers.
- Over-crediting earnings lifted by high rates, wider spreads, or volatility-inflated margin balances.

## Operational, Cyber, and Regulatory Traps

- Underweighting outages, data latency, failed migrations, cyber incidents, resilience underinvestment, or data-integrity failures because the company looks asset-light.
- Assuming market-data or index pricing power is unlimited despite regulatory review, customer consolidation, redistribution disputes, substitution, or benchmark-transition risk.
- Missing product-specific rules: position limits, transaction taxes, clearing mandates, tick-size reforms, open-access/interoperability, market-structure reviews, benchmark governance, and market-data fee litigation.
- Ignoring structural subordination between the analyzed security and regulated or ring-fenced cash flows.

## Never Do This

- Do not compare exchanges on headline volume growth without normalizing product mix, contract value, fee tier, rebates, and net capture.
- Do not capitalize peak volatility, collateral income, or issuance/listings bursts as normal recurring revenue.
- Do not call market-data, index, or workflow revenue "SaaS-like" without checking churn, contract duration, price regulation, customer concentration, and activity sensitivity.
- Do not underwrite CCP economics without a stress-liquidity, default-management, and mutualization analysis.
