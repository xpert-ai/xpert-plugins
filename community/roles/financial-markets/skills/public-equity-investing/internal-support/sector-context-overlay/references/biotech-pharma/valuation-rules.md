# Valuation Rules

Deferred reference for `sector-context-overlay` biotech-pharma sector lens. Load only when the task needs valuation, public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff, or Credit Markets handoff rules.

## Use life-sciences valuation, not generic healthcare shortcuts

## Equity valuation hierarchy

1. Risk-adjusted NPV or probability-weighted sum-of-the-parts for clinical-stage and pipeline-driven companies.
2. Hybrid sum-of-the-parts for companies with both a commercial base business and meaningful pipeline optionality.
3. EV / sales or EV / gross profit for early commercial companies where margins are not yet stable but launch quality matters.
4. DCF, FCF yield, or earnings-based frameworks for mature diversified pharma once LOE and pipeline replacement are modeled explicitly.
5. Yield and DCF analysis for royalty and milestone-finance vehicles after mapping duration and expiry.

### Valuation rules
- Value the label and the actual treated-patient funnel, not a generic TAM slide.
- Use higher probability weights only when data are controlled, clinically meaningful, manufacturable, and regulatorily clean.
- Peak-sales assumptions must be built from patient flow, access, persistence, share, net price, and geography, not from management aspiration.
- For diversified pharma, split cash-cow franchises, launch assets, business-development dependence, and patent-cliff exposures rather than applying one multiple to all earnings.
- Revenue multiples are less useful when gross-to-net is moving, launch demand is channel-loaded, or the company mixes product revenue with collaboration revenue.
- Royalty streams should be valued on durability and legal priority, not just on current yield.

## Credit valuation hierarchy

1. Liquidity runway and access to financing.
2. Asset quality and durability of cash-generating products or royalties.
3. Timing mismatch between debt maturities and major catalysts.
4. Royalty, lien, and covenant structure.
5. Recovery realism under a failed readout, CRL, or faster-than-expected erosion event.
