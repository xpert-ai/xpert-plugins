# Source Hierarchy And Diligence

Deferred reference for `Exchanges Market Infrastructure Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Source hierarchy and reconciliation rules

Use this source order whenever possible:
1. latest earnings release and earnings presentation for current management framing
2. annual report or 10-K or 20-F for segment definitions, accounting, legal structure, and risks
3. monthly or quarterly volume and capture reports for current activity and mix
4. clearinghouse rulebooks and risk disclosures for margin, collateral, eligible assets, and default waterfalls
5. fee schedules and product documentation for monetization details
6. only then use third-party market-share summaries, and always reconcile company-defined denominators first

## Reconciliation rules

- Never compare ARR, market share, ADV, ADNV, RPC, net capture, or FPM across companies without checking definitions.
- Confirm whether volume is contract-based, notional-based, share-based, single-counted, or double-counted.
- Strip out pass-through items, rebates, liquidity payments, royalty fees, and direct trading costs before drawing conclusions about economics.
- If management reports preliminary capture or fee metrics, treat them as subject to change until the quarter closes.
- If the company changed methodology because of acquisitions, restatements, or product regrouping, rebuild comparability yourself.
## Exchange and market infrastructure-specific diligence requests

Request or reconstruct the following whenever possible:
- segment revenue bridge between volume, price, recurring revenue, and balance-related economics
- product-level concentration map showing what actually drives EBITDA
- monthly activity history by product and by capture metric
- clearing and collateral economics, including who owns the spread and how balances are invested
- member and client concentration, especially for CCPs and institutional platforms
- regulatory matters, fee litigation, and material market-structure debates
- technology roadmap, outage history, cyber posture, and cloud-migration status
- acquisition scorecard for synergy realization and cross-sell credibility
- entity-level debt map and any restrictions on upstreaming cash
