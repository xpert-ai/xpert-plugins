# Output Overlays And Handoffs

Deferred reference for `Oil Gas E&P Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Adapt the lens to the investor mandate

## Long-short fundamental
Focus on what the market is mispricing about reserve quality, inventory duration, maintenance capital, capital allocation, or balance-sheet resilience.

## Event-driven
Prioritize catalysts: M&A, acreage swaps, asset sales, reserve report surprises, spacing updates, borrowing-base redeterminations, debt exchanges, activist pressure, major discoveries, project start-ups, or regulatory and pipeline events.

## Macro
Emphasize commodity curves, OPEC+ and supply response, LNG demand, basis and takeaway, service-cost inflation, interest rates, and how those macro variables interact with company-specific hedging and capital allocation.

## Quant / systematic
Standardize the most predictive variables: oil mix, realized-price quality, reserve replacement, inventory duration, maintenance-capital burden, margin per BOE, leverage, hedge coverage, and free-cash-flow yield.

## Credit Markets handoff / equity-risk debt context
Focus on PDP value, collateral leakage, borrowing-base risk, transport and other fixed commitments, covenant headroom, maturity wall, and recovery under downside decks.

Credit Markets owns debt-security, covenant-package, recovery-waterfall, and credit-instrument analysis. In Public Equity Investing, use this section only to translate those risks into common-equity downside, valuation, catalyst, sizing, hedge, or re-underwrite implications.

## Growth-oriented investor
Translate growth into per-share reserve value, per-share production value, and sustainable free cash flow. Favor companies that can grow without exhausting core inventory or levering the balance sheet.
## Output overlays when this skill is used with common finance tasks

## With an earnings deep dive
Always add:
- production bridge by commodity and basin
- reserve and production replacement bridge
- realized-price and differential table
- hedge and transport exposure summary
- maintenance-capital versus growth-capital framing
- leverage, liquidity, and next 12 to 24 month catalyst table

## With an investment memo
Always include:
- e&p archetype and basin classification
- thesis by inventory, price realization, cost position, and balance sheet
- variant perception on reserve quality, maintenance capital, or commodity sensitivity
- downside NAV and downside liquidity case
- disconfirming evidence
- security-specific recommendation with claim-position discussion

## With a financial model
Always produce:
- production schedules by commodity and basin
- realized-price and hedge schedules
- LOE, GPT, production-tax, and G&A schedules
- capex schedule split between maintenance and growth logic
- reserve or inventory roll-forward logic
- free-cash-flow and leverage outputs
- NAV and commodity sensitivity tables

## When debt or structured credit work is requested, route to Credit Markets
Always include:
- PDP reserve-value framing and downside collateral discussion
- maturity, liquidity, and refinancing path
- downside strip and basis stress
- transport and fixed-obligation burden
- abandonment-liability and collateral-leakage discussion

Do not turn the sector overlay into a debt-security recommendation. Route instrument-level yield/spread, claim priority, covenant enforcement, recovery waterfall, and restructuring work to Credit Markets.

## Default writing style

Write like a veteran upstream analyst:
- lead with rock quality, reserve quality, and inventory duration, not headline production growth
- separate molecules before combining them
- separate benchmark prices from realized prices
- separate PDP durability from undeveloped promise
- translate management optimism into maintenance capital, reserve replacement, and per-share value consequences
- be skeptical of acreage maps, type curves, and BOE metrics until they reconcile to cash flow and reserve quality

When unsure, prioritize reserve quality, inventory depth, realized-price quality, maintenance-capital burden, and balance-sheet resilience over one quarter of strong commodity prices or management volume guidance.

## Mandate Overlay Sections

### Long-only

Focus on thesis durability, benchmark-relative risk, liquidity, downside capture, add/trim path, and whether the sector KPI path supports a core or satellite position.

### Long/short

Focus on variant wedge, catalyst timing, shortability/borrow where relevant, factor crowding, pair/hedge logic, and cover or exit triggers.

### Sell-side / initiation

Focus on rating/target-price debate, estimate revision bridge, Street debate, sector valuation convention, and risks to rating or target.

### ETF / index

Focus on index membership, constituent weight, liquidity, float, passive ownership, factor/sector exposure, rebalance risk, and flow-vs-ADV where relevant.

### Public equity diligence

Focus on business quality, governance, accounting quality, capital allocation, management credibility, source gaps, and next diligence source.

### Model / valuation

Focus on the sector driver that changes estimates, multiple, risk premium, or terminal value. Identify the metric that would force the model to move.

### Earnings / catalyst

Focus on which sector KPI changes the forward estimate path, narrative, multiple, position size, downside, liquidity, or event probability.

### Source discipline

State which sector claims are sourced, stale, inferred, or missing. Flag unsupported precision and use `missing_evidence` for material gaps.
