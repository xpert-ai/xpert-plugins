# Valuation Rules

Deferred reference for `sector-context-overlay` reits sector lens. Load only when the task needs valuation, public-equity security context and Credit Markets handoff, or Credit Markets handoff rules.

## Use REIT-specific valuation, not generic corporate valuation

## Equity valuation hierarchy

### Net lease, industrial, apartments, shopping center, self-storage, towers, and many high-quality equity REITs
Use these lenses first:
1. price / AFFO or price / cash earnings
2. price / Nareit FFO
3. premium or discount to NAV
4. implied cap rate versus private-market value and growth

### Hotel, office, mall, healthcare operating, and capex-heavy or transition situations
Use these lenses first:
1. NAV or downside NAV
2. EV / EBITDAre or property cash flow
3. AFFO only after heavy capex normalization
4. dividend yield only as a secondary check, never as the thesis

### Data center and specialty growth REITs
Use these lenses first:
1. AFFO or FFO plus backlog- or development-adjusted growth
2. NAV adjusted for development pipeline and capital intensity
3. private-market replacement cost and power or capacity constraints where relevant

### Valuation rules
- Compare multiples only within similar lease models, capex burdens, and growth profiles.
- A low FFO multiple can be deserved if rollover risk, TI/LC burden, capex, or refinancing stress is high.
- A premium multiple can be deserved if the REIT has durable embedded rent growth, scarce development runway, superior balance-sheet flexibility, and low recurring cash leakage.
- Never let the headline dividend yield substitute for NAV, leverage, or AFFO quality.
- When using NAV, be explicit about cap rates, mark-to-market rents, stabilized occupancy, G&A, recurring capex, and debt or preferred claims.
- Price/FFO is useful; price/AFFO is often better; neither is sufficient without a NAV cross-check.

## Debt valuation hierarchy
- asset coverage and downside NAV
- unsecured versus secured position and recovery
- maturity and refinancing risk
- liquidity and covenant headroom
- tenant or operator stress path and NOI resilience
