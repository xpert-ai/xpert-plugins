# KPI Cheat Sheet

Deferred reference for `REIT Sector Analysis`. Load this only when the task needs this level of sector detail.

## Property Cash-Flow Metric Map

## 1) Property type, lease model, and economic unit of account
### Metrics to track
- same-store NOI or equivalent operating measure
- occupancy, leased rate, retention, and average remaining lease term
- cash rent change, GAAP rent change, or blended lease-rate growth
- tenant concentration and top-tenant exposure
- property mix by sector, market, and asset quality
- proportion of contractual escalators fixed versus CPI-linked
- percentage of revenue from operating assets versus contractual rent

## 2) Portfolio quality, market selection, and asset-level moat
### Metrics to track
- same-store pool size and composition
- top markets and concentration by NOI
- tenant concentration by annualized base rent or NOI
- weighted average lease term, expiration schedule, and percent of NOI rolling by year
- sales per square foot, occupancy cost, or tenant coverage where relevant
- same-store occupancy and leased-to-occupied spread
- new supply as a percent of market inventory where relevant
- asset age, redevelopment pipeline, and major capex needs

## 3) Leasing, occupancy, same-store NOI, and embedded growth
### Metrics to track
- same-store NOI growth, revenue growth, and expense growth
- cash leasing spreads and GAAP leasing spreads
- leased rate, occupied rate, and retention
- bad debt, concessions, and free-rent burn-off
- rent per square foot, rent per occupied square foot, or rate per unit
- TI/LC burden and downtime where relevant
- RevPAR, ADR, Revenue Per Occupied Room, or hotel EBITDA margin where relevant
- signed-not-open backlog, commencements, or organic tenant billings where relevant

## 4) External growth, development, redevelopment, and capital allocation
### Metrics to track
- acquisition cap rate or initial cash yield
- development yield on cost, stabilization timeline, and prelease percentage
- disposition cap rate and gains
- retained cash after dividend
- acquisition and development spend versus funding sources
- equity issuance, ATM usage, forward equity, JV capital, and debt financing
- percentage of AFFO growth coming from same-store versus external sources

## 5) Balance sheet, leverage, liquidity, and cost of capital
### Metrics to track
- net debt to EBITDAre or adjusted EBITDAre
- fixed-charge coverage and interest coverage
- weighted average interest rate and weighted average term to maturity
- fixed versus floating debt mix
- secured debt as a percent of gross assets
- unencumbered NOI and unsecured debt coverage
- available liquidity, revolver capacity, and near-term maturity ladder
- debt to enterprise value or debt to gross assets where relevant

## 6) FFO, AFFO, dividend safety, and earnings-quality normalization
### Metrics to track
- Nareit FFO per share and growth
- AFFO or CAD per share and growth
- dividend payout as a percent of AFFO or CAD
- recurring capex plus TI/LC as a percent of NOI or revenue
- straight-line rent as a percent of revenue
- same-store NOI contribution versus acquisition or development contribution
- retained cash after dividends
