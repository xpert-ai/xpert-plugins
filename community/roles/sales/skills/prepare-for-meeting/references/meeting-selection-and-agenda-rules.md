# Meeting Selection And Agenda Rules

Use this reference only when the main skill instructions leave meeting qualification, agenda scope, attendee enrichment, or asset choice ambiguous.

## Qualification Edge Cases

- For single-meeting briefs and daily digests, require at least one external attendee or another explicit account/customer signal.
- Reject routine internal meetings, recruiting meetings, 1:1s, performance conversations, and operational syncs unless the user explicitly asked for them or the invite evidence shows clear business stakes.
- In scheduled most-important mode, select a non-customer meeting only when no customer/account meeting qualifies and the work meeting is non-routine, high-impact, and prep would materially improve the user's next action.

## Account Or Workstream Resolution

- Resolve the likely account from external attendee domains when the user did not name one.
- If multiple external companies appear, anchor on the company most central to the request and call out the others.
- If no credible account anchor can be resolved for a customer-mode brief, stop and report the gap instead of improvising a customer brief.
- For a scheduled non-customer meeting, resolve the workstream from title, invite body, attendees, internal docs, and recent internal messages instead of inventing an account anchor.
- When CRM has multiple opportunities for the account, prefer the one that matches the user ask, meeting title or invite topic, attendee roles, product or solution aliases, and recent activity.
- Do not let an unrelated same-account opportunity drive the brief. Mention it only as excluded or conflicting context when that helps the seller.

## Agenda Scope

Use this priority order for `Focused Agenda`:

1. Explicit user ask or named account/topic.
2. Calendar title, invite body, attached agenda, and attendee list.
3. Recent external email or `external_messaging` about the meeting.
4. Prior meeting notes with the same customer and topic.
5. `internal_messaging` that references the upcoming meeting, a same-day customer call, the same attendees, or a current blocker.
6. CRM or document-store items with current dates, owners, or open next actions.
7. Broad account-plan strategy, only as background or when directly tied to the signals above.

Agenda phrasing rules:

- Prefer phrasing that names the decision to make, not just the topic to discuss.
- If the title points to a narrow implementation, pilot, support escalation, security review, procurement step, or launch review, make that topic the agenda spine.
- Move broader commercial, executive, product, or partnership context into background unless it changes a decision needed in this meeting.
- Use internal sales-review or prep-call transcripts as internal continuity when they match the account and topic; do not present them as customer history unless the source shows customer participation.

## Multi-Meeting Handling

- In a daily digest, order qualifying meetings chronologically.
- Keep separate briefs for separate meetings, even when they involve the same account.
- Reuse supported account framing across same-account meetings, but do not merge distinct meetings into one summary.

## Enrichment And Assets

- Add public attendee title and company only from high-confidence LinkedIn/profile matches; never infer titles from names alone, and omit weak or ambiguous matches.
- Prefer official company pages, official product/customer pages, filings, press releases, investor materials, and reputable news for public account enrichment.
- Use public context only when it sharpens the meeting background, questions, risks, or close.
- Recommend up to 3 existing assets only when there is evidence they exist and would help this meeting.
