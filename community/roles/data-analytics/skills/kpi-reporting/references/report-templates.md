# Report Templates

Use this reference after the output format is selected. These are starting patterns, not mandatory layouts.

These templates shape the final artifact only. Complete the KPI Reporting workflow first; analysis, validation, and operating implications come from that workflow.

## Inline Written Update

Use for a short chat, email, or memo-style update. Lead with the answer, then include the headline actuals, comparison or pacing context, main drivers, and the operating implication or next step.

## Document Or Report

Route document and report outputs through $build-report. Use this reference to shape the KPI content passed into that workflow: summary, KPI snapshot, driver analysis, business context, implications, caveats, and reader-relevant source or calculation notes.

## Single Slide

Use for one concise readout. Follow the user's requested surface: $build-report for a report artifact or HTML, or the native presentation surface when the user needs a slide file. Ask which path they want when unclear. A single-slide story should have one main message, a compact view of the headline metrics, and only the driver, risk, or action detail needed to support that message.

## Executive Business Review Deck

Use for larger executive readouts, such as weekly, monthly, or quarterly business reviews. Follow the user's requested surface: $build-report for a report artifact or HTML, or the native presentation surface when the user needs a deck file. Ask which path they want when unclear.

- Give a concise executive summary of overall status, what changed, and what needs attention.
- Use KPI cards, a scorecard, or another compact summary format that makes headline performance easy to scan; move dense detail into supporting sections.
- Organize the body around the main drivers of performance. Each section should connect the driver evidence to the operating takeaway, with action, risk, or follow-up when one is warranted.
- Keep detailed evidence, large tables, and deeper cuts in supporting sections or an appendix.
- Include prior follow-ups when they are useful for a recurring readout, with ownership and current status.
- End with the prioritized actions or open questions leaders need to resolve.

## Scorecard Or KPI Card Component

Use a compact scorecard when the reader needs to compare multiple KPIs at once. Include the actual, relevant comparison, target or pacing context when available, status, and a short driver or interpretation. Expand the explanation below the table when the driver story needs more room.
